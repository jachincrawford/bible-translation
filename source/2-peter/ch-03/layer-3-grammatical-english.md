**v.1**
This already, beloved, a second letter I write to you, in which I stir up your sincere mind by way of reminder,

*Notes:*
- ταύτην ἤδη δευτέραν: "this already a second" — the demonstrative and ordinal are straightforward; this establishes 2 Peter as a sequel to a prior letter (presumably 1 Peter, though the referent is debated) [INTERP-RISK: the identification of the "first letter" is not explicitly stated in the Greek]
- εἰλικρινῆ: "sincere/pure" — from εἱλικρινής = tested by sunlight, found genuine; applied to διάνοιαν = the faculty of understanding/mind
- διεγείρω: "stir up/awaken" — same verb as 1:13; the author's explicit self-reference to the stirring-up motif links ch.3 to ch.1
- ἐν ὑπομνήσει: "by way of reminder" — the dative of means; the purpose of the letter is recollection, not new revelation

---

**v.2**
to remember the words spoken before by the holy prophets and the commandment of the Lord and Savior through your apostles.

*Notes:*
- μνησθῆναι: aorist passive infinitive of μιμνῄσκομαι = "to remember/to be reminded" — purpose infinitive dependent on "stir up" (v.1)
- τῶν προειρημένων ῥημάτων: "the words spoken before" — perfect passive participle of προλέγω; the prophetic words are already on record
- ὑπὸ τῶν ἁγίων προφητῶν: "by the holy prophets" — OT prophets are the referent
- τῆς τῶν ἀποστόλων ὑμῶν ἐντολῆς τοῦ Κυρίου καὶ Σωτῆρος: "the commandment of the Lord and Savior through your apostles" [INTERP-RISK: the genitive chain is syntactically complex — "commandment of the Lord and Savior" is the content; "through your apostles" is the channel of transmission; the apostles mediate the Lord's command, not issue their own]

**v.3**
knowing this first, that in the last days scoffers will come with scoffing [NT-HAPAX: ἐμπαιγμονή — only NT occurrence; cognate of ἐμπαίζω = "to mock/ridicule"], walking according to their own desires,

*Notes:*
- γινώσκοντες πρῶτον: "knowing this first" — the priority marker signals that what follows is the foundational warning
- ἐπ᾽ ἐσχάτων τῶν ἡμερῶν: "in the last of the days" — the prepositional phrase is standard eschatological formula
- ἐμπαῖκται: "scoffers/mockers" — agents of ridicule; the noun appears only here and Jude 18 in the NT
- ἐμπαιγμονῇ [NT-HAPAX]: "with scoffing/with mockery" — only NT occurrence; the dative of manner intensifies the characterization: they come scoffing with scoffing; cognate accusative-like construction
- κατὰ τὰς ἰδίας ἐπιθυμίας αὐτῶν πορευόμενοι: "walking according to their own desires" — the double possessive (ἰδίας + αὐτῶν) is emphatic

**v.4**
and saying, "Where is the promise of his coming? For since the fathers fell asleep, all things continue as they were from the beginning of creation."

*Notes:*
- ποῦ ἐστιν: "where is" — the skeptical interrogative; a rhetorical challenge, not a genuine question
- ἡ ἐπαγγελία τῆς παρουσίας αὐτοῦ: "the promise of his coming" — παρουσία = arrival/presence; the technical term for Christ's return
- ἀφ᾽ ἧς οἱ πατέρες ἐκοιμήθησαν: "since the fathers fell asleep" — κοιμάω = "to sleep" as a euphemism for death; "the fathers" = either the OT patriarchs or the first generation of Christians [INTERP-RISK: the referent of "the fathers" is debated — OT patriarchs or early Christian leaders; "fathers" retained without resolution]
- πάντα οὕτως διαμένει: "all things continue as they were" — the scoffers' argument is uniformitarian: the world is unchanged, therefore the promise has failed
- ἀπ᾽ ἀρχῆς κτίσεως: "from the beginning of creation" — the scope of the scoffers' claim extends back to the cosmological beginning

---

**v.5**
For this escapes their notice, willfully, that the heavens existed long ago and the earth was formed out of water and through water by the word of God,

*Notes:*
- λανθάνει γὰρ αὐτοὺς τοῦτο θέλοντας: "for this escapes them willfully" — the participle θέλοντας ("willfully/willingly") modifies the subject of ignorance; the ignorance is deliberate, not accidental [INTERP-RISK: the placement of θέλοντας is load-bearing — "willfully ignorant" vs. "they are willing that this escape them"; the deliberate-ignorance reading is standard but the construction is unusual]
- ἔκπαλαι: "long ago" — rare adverb; also appears at 2:3; the antiquity of the heavens is set against the scoffers' claim about the beginning of creation
- γῆ ἐξ ὕδατος καὶ δι᾽ ὕδατος: "the earth out of water and through water" — the double prepositional phrase (ἐξ + διά) emphasizes water's role in both origin and means of the earth's formation; echoes Gen 1:2, 6–10 [INTERP-RISK: the precise cosmological model implied — separation of waters or earth arising from waters — is not specified further; rendered literally]
- τῷ τοῦ Θεοῦ λόγῳ: "by the word of God" — dative of instrument/agency; the divine word as the active agent of creation; cf. Gen 1:3 "And God said"
- συνεστῶσα: perfect active participle of συνίστημι = "having been constituted/formed/held together" — the earth was brought into coherent existence and continues to exist by the divine word

---

**v.6**
through which the world of that time, having been flooded with water, perished.

*Notes:*
- δι᾽ ὧν: "through which" — the antecedent is disputed: "through which [waters]" or "through which [word and water together]" [INTERP-RISK: the plural relative pronoun ὧν could refer to the waters (plural), or to "water and word" taken together; "through which" is retained without resolution]
- ὁ τότε κόσμος: "the world of that time" — the pre-diluvian world is distinguished from the present world (v.7)
- ὕδατι κατακλυσθείς: "having been flooded with water" — aorist passive participle of κατακλύζω; the noun κατακλυσμός = "flood/deluge" (cf. Matt 24:38–39)
- ἀπώλετο: "perished" — 2nd aorist middle of ἀπόλλυμι; the destruction of the pre-flood world by water establishes the precedent for future destruction (v.7)

---

**v.7**
But the present heavens and earth by the same word have been stored up for fire, being kept for the day of judgment and destruction of ungodly men.

*Notes:*
- οἱ δὲ νῦν οὐρανοὶ καὶ ἡ γῆ: "the present heavens and earth" — the δέ marks the contrast with the pre-flood world (v.6); "present" = νῦν
- τῷ αὐτῷ λόγῳ: "by the same word" — the same divine word that created (v.5) and destroyed (v.6) now preserves the present cosmos for a future purpose
- τεθησαυρισμένοι εἰσίν: "have been stored up" — perfect passive periphrastic of θησαυρίζω = "to treasure up/store up"; the present heavens are a stored reserve [INTERP-RISK: the metaphor of "storing up" — whether the heavens are stored for fire (fire is the instrument) or stored up as fuel for fire — is ambiguous; "stored up for fire" rendered literally]
- πυρί: "for fire" — dative; the fire is the future agent of destruction, as water was the past agent (v.6)
- τηρούμενοι: "being kept" — present passive participle; the τηρέω motif continues from ch. 2 (2:4, 9, 17); divine custody for future judgment
- εἰς ἡμέραν κρίσεως καὶ ἀπωλείας τῶν ἀσεβῶν ἀνθρώπων: "for the day of judgment and destruction of ungodly men" — the purpose of the keeping; ἀσεβῶν echoes the ἀσεβ- word group throughout 2 Peter (2:5, 6; 3:7)

---

**v.8**
But do not let this one thing escape your notice, beloved, that one day with the Lord is as a thousand years and a thousand years as one day.

*Notes:*
- ἓν δὲ τοῦτο μὴ λανθανέτω ὑμᾶς: "do not let this one thing escape your notice" — the verb λανθάνω echoes v.5 (the scoffers' willful ignorance); the believers must not share the scoffers' blindness
- ἀγαπητοί: "beloved" — vocative; the third of four occurrences in ch. 3 (vv.1, 8, 14, 17); marks the pastoral tone
- μία ἡμέρα παρὰ Κυρίῳ ὡς χίλια ἔτη καὶ χίλια ἔτη ὡς ἡμέρα μία: "one day with the Lord is as a thousand years and a thousand years as one day" — allusion to Ps 90:4 (LXX 89:4); the chiastic structure (day → thousand years → thousand years → day) is rhetorically deliberate; divine temporality is incommensurable with human reckoning [INTERP-RISK: the force of ὡς = "as/like" — simile, not equation; the passage asserts qualitative incommensurability, not mathematical equivalence]

---

**v.9**
The Lord is not slow concerning the promise, as some consider slowness, but is patient toward you, not wishing any to perish but all to come to repentance.

*Notes:*
- οὐ βραδύνει Κύριος τῆς ἐπαγγελίας: "the Lord is not slow concerning the promise" — βραδύνω = "to be slow/to delay"; the genitive τῆς ἐπαγγελίας is genitive of reference
- ὥς τινες βραδύτητα ἡγοῦνται: "as some consider slowness" — the scoffers' perception (v.4) is explicitly referenced and refuted
- μακροθυμεῖ εἰς ὑμᾶς: "is patient toward you" — μακροθυμέω = "to be long-tempered/patient"; the divine delay is reinterpreted as divine patience [INTERP-RISK: εἰς ὑμᾶς = "toward you" — the scope of "you" (the believing community specifically, or humanity broadly) affects the theological import; rendered literally]
- μὴ βουλόμενός τινας ἀπολέσθαι: "not wishing any to perish" — βούλομαι = "to will/wish/purpose"; the scope of τινας ("any") is debated [INTERP-RISK: "any" — universal scope or limited to "you" (the addressees)? The grammar supports both readings; rendered literally without resolution]
- ἀλλὰ πάντας εἰς μετάνοιαν χωρῆσαι: "but all to come to repentance" — χωρέω = "to make room/to come forward"; μετάνοια = "a change of mind/repentance"

**v.10**
But the day of the Lord will come as a thief, in which the heavens will pass away with a roar [NT-HAPAX: ῥοιζηδόν — only NT occurrence; onomatopoeic adverb = "with a rushing/whizzing/hissing sound"], and the elements will be dissolved with intense heat [INTERP-RISK: στοιχεῖα = "elements" — the referent is disputed: physical elements (earth, water, air, fire), heavenly bodies (stars, planets), or spiritual/elemental powers; "elements" is retained as the least interpretive gloss], and the earth and the works in it will be found [INTERP-RISK: εὑρεθήσεται = "will be found/discovered" — this is the most difficult textual variant in the letter; the N1904 reading εὑρεθήσεται ("will be found") is the lectio difficilior; variant readings include κατακαήσεται ("will be burned up"), οὐχ εὑρεθήσεται ("will not be found"), and ἀφανισθήσονται ("will vanish"); "will be found" retained per N1904].

*Notes:*
- ἥξει δὲ ἡμέρα Κυρίου ὡς κλέπτης: "the day of the Lord will come as a thief" — the simile emphasizes unexpectedness, not stealth; cf. 1 Thess 5:2; Matt 24:43
- ῥοιζηδόν [NT-HAPAX]: adverb derived from ῥοῖζος = "rushing noise/whizzing sound"; onomatopoeic; the dissolution is audible and violent; only NT occurrence
- στοιχεῖα [INTERP-RISK: "elements" — in Hellenistic usage this term ranged from (a) physical elements/components of matter, (b) heavenly bodies/celestial objects, (c) foundational principles, to (d) spiritual powers (cf. Gal 4:3, 9; Col 2:8, 20); Peter's usage here and in v.12 appears to be cosmological; "elements" retained literally; Layer 4 may specify]
- καυσούμενα: present passive participle of καυσόω = "being burned with intense heat" — the burning is in progress
- εὑρεθήσεται [INTERP-RISK: future passive of εὑρίσκω = "will be found/discovered"; this is the crux interpretum of the entire letter; the meaning — that the earth and its works "will be found" (i.e., laid bare, exposed, discovered by God's judgment) — is theologically coherent but textually contested; N1904 reading retained; major variants: κατακαήσεται (P72, many minuscules), οὐχ εὑρεθήσεται (Codex Sinaiticus corrector), ἀφανισθήσονται (some witnesses)]

**v.11**
Since all these things are being dissolved, what sort of persons ought you to be in holy conduct and godliness,

*Notes:*
- τούτων οὖν πάντων λυομένων: "since all these things are being dissolved" — genitive absolute with present passive participle; the dissolution is treated as in process (or certain); the cosmological dissolution grounds the ethical exhortation
- ποταποὺς δεῖ ὑπάρχειν ὑμᾶς: "what sort of persons ought you to be" — ποταπός = "of what sort/of what kind"; rhetorical question expecting an emphatic answer; δεῖ = "it is necessary"
- ἐν ἁγίαις ἀναστροφαῖς καὶ εὐσεβείαις: "in holy conduct and godliness" — the plurals (ἀναστροφαῖς, εὐσεβείαις) are unusual [INTERP-RISK: the plural forms may indicate multiple acts/instances of holy conduct and godliness, or may be a stylistic plural; English singulars are equally defensible; plurals retained]

**v.12**
looking for and hastening the coming of the day of God [INTERP-RISK: σπεύδοντας = "hastening" — the participle may mean (a) "hastening" (transitive: actively speeding the arrival of the day) or (b) "earnestly desiring/eagerly awaiting" (intransitive); if (a), human conduct can accelerate the eschatological timetable; if (b), it is parallel to "looking for"; the transitive reading "hastening" is the more difficult and is retained], because of which the heavens, being set on fire, will be dissolved, and the elements, burning with intense heat, are melting!

*Notes:*
- προσδοκῶντας: "looking for/expecting" — present active participle; the forward-looking posture of the believer
- σπεύδοντας [INTERP-RISK: if transitive "hastening," the day of God can be hastened by repentance and holy living (cf. Acts 3:19–20; rabbinic traditions of Israel hastening the Messiah through obedience); if intransitive "eagerly desiring," the force is purely anticipatory; both readings are grammatically possible; "hastening" retained as the more theologically distinctive reading]
- τὴν παρουσίαν τῆς τοῦ Θεοῦ ἡμέρας: "the coming of the day of God" — note "day of God" (not "day of the Lord" as in v.10); the shift may be deliberate [INTERP-RISK: minor — "day of God" vs. "day of the Lord" — the distinction may be merely stylistic or may carry theological weight]
- πυρούμενοι: present passive participle of πυρόω = "being set on fire" — the heavens are ignited
- τήκεται: present passive of τήκω = "is melting/dissolving" — a different verb from λύω (v.10, 11), adding the image of melting under extreme heat
- στοιχεῖα: "elements" — same term as v.10; same interpretive range applies

**v.13**
But according to his promise we are looking for new heavens and a new earth, in which righteousness dwells.

*Notes:*
- κατὰ δὲ τὸ ἐπάγγελμα αὐτοῦ: "according to his promise" — ἐπάγγελμα = the promise itself (noun form), not the act of promising; the referent is Isa 65:17; 66:22
- καινοὺς οὐρανοὺς καὶ γῆν καινήν: "new heavens and a new earth" — καινός = "new" in quality (renewed/fresh), not merely νέος (new in time); the adjective is positioned emphatically in both phrases
- προσδοκῶμεν: "we are looking for" — present active indicative (or subjunctive); same verb as v.12 (προσδοκῶντας); the expectation is ongoing
- ἐν οἷς δικαιοσύνη κατοικεῖ: "in which righteousness dwells" — κατοικέω = to settle/dwell permanently (not merely παροικέω = to sojourn); righteousness will be a permanent resident, not a visitor

**v.14**
Therefore, beloved, since you are looking for these things, be diligent to be found by him spotless and blameless, in peace,

*Notes:*
- διό: "therefore" — the inferential conjunction draws the ethical conclusion from the eschatological expectation (vv.10–13)
- ταῦτα προσδοκῶντες: "since you are looking for these things" — the third use of προσδοκάω in this chapter (vv.12, 13, 14)
- σπουδάσατε: aorist imperative of σπουδάζω = "be diligent/make every effort" — cf. 1:5 (same verb); the urgency is emphatic
- ἄσπιλοι καὶ ἀμώμητοι: "spotless and blameless" — cf. 2:13 (σπίλοι καὶ μῶμοι = "stains and blemishes" of the false teachers); the believers are to be the precise antithesis of the false teachers; the lexical contrast is deliberate
- εὑρεθῆναι αὐτῷ: "to be found by him" — the verb εὑρίσκω echoes v.10 (εὑρεθήσεται); the earth will be "found" and the believers are to be "found" — the verbal echo links cosmological and personal judgment
- ἐν εἰρήνῃ: "in peace" — the closing note; peace as the condition of the believer at the parousia

**v.15**
and consider the patience of our Lord as salvation, just as also our beloved brother Paul, according to the wisdom given to him, wrote to you,

*Notes:*
- τὴν τοῦ Κυρίου ἡμῶν μακροθυμίαν σωτηρίαν ἡγεῖσθε: "consider the patience of our Lord as salvation" — the imperative ἡγεῖσθε = "consider/regard"; the delay (μακροθυμία, cf. v.9) is reframed as saving patience, not negligence
- ὁ ἀγαπητὸς ἡμῶν ἀδελφὸς Παῦλος: "our beloved brother Paul" — the identification of Paul as a fellow apostle and brother; the designation ἀγαπητός echoes the address to the readers (vv.1, 8, 14, 17)
- κατὰ τὴν δοθεῖσαν αὐτῷ σοφίαν: "according to the wisdom given to him" — the passive δοθεῖσαν (aorist passive participle of δίδωμι) indicates divine gifting; Paul's wisdom is a received endowment
- ἔγραψεν ὑμῖν: "wrote to you" — aorist; the specific Pauline letter referred to is not identified [INTERP-RISK: which Pauline letter is meant — Romans, 1 Thessalonians, or another — is not determinable from the text alone; rendered without specification]

**v.16**
as also in all his letters, speaking in them concerning these things, in which are some things hard to understand [NT-HAPAX: δυσνόητα — only NT occurrence; compound: δυσ- (difficult) + νοέω (to perceive/understand)], which the unlearned [NT-HAPAX: ἀμαθεῖς — only NT occurrence; from ἀ- (privative) + μανθάνω (to learn)] and unstable distort [NT-HAPAX: στρεβλώσουσιν — only NT occurrence; from στρεβλόω = "to twist on the rack/to torture/to distort"], as they do also the rest of the Scriptures, to their own destruction.

*Notes:*
- ὡς καὶ ἐν πάσαις ταῖς ἐπιστολαῖς: "as also in all his letters" [INTERP-RISK: "all the letters" — the scope is debated; does this imply a collected Pauline corpus already in circulation, or merely the letters known to the author and audience? The phrase presupposes knowledge of multiple Pauline letters; "all his letters" retained literally]
- λαλῶν ἐν αὐταῖς περὶ τούτων: "speaking in them concerning these things" — the eschatological themes of ch. 3 are said to appear in Paul's letters
- δυσνόητα [NT-HAPAX]: "hard to understand" — the compound adjective acknowledges genuine difficulty in Paul's writings; only NT occurrence
- ἀμαθεῖς [NT-HAPAX]: "unlearned/ignorant" — only NT occurrence; those who lack proper instruction or training
- ἀστήρικτοι: "unstable" — same adjective as 2:14; those who are not firmly grounded
- στρεβλοῦσιν [NT-HAPAX]: "distort/twist" — the verb is drawn from the torture rack (στρέβλη); the image is of twisting something out of its natural shape; only NT occurrence
- τὰς λοιπὰς γραφάς: "the rest of the Scriptures" — Paul's letters are placed alongside "the Scriptures" (γραφαί); this is load-bearing for the canonical status of Pauline letters [INTERP-RISK: does "the rest of the Scriptures" imply that Paul's letters are themselves Scripture, or merely that the same people also distort the Scriptures? The Greek λοιπάς ("the rest/remaining") most naturally implies inclusion — they distort Paul's letters as they do the rest of the Scriptures, i.e., Paul's letters are in the same category; rendered literally]
- πρὸς τὴν ἰδίαν αὐτῶν ἀπώλειαν: "to their own destruction" — the double possessive (ἰδίαν + αὐτῶν) emphatic; the distortion rebounds on the distorters

**v.17**
You therefore, beloved, knowing this beforehand, be on your guard so that you are not carried away by the error of unprincipled men and fall from your own steadfastness [NT-HAPAX: στηριγμοῦ — only NT occurrence; from στηρίζω = "to establish/set firmly"],

*Notes:*
- ὑμεῖς οὖν ἀγαπητοί: "you therefore, beloved" — the emphatic pronoun ὑμεῖς + fourth and final ἀγαπητοί in ch. 3; the pastoral urgency is at its peak
- προγινώσκοντες: "knowing this beforehand" — the foreknowledge of the danger is the ground of the exhortation; the believers have been warned (the letter itself constitutes the warning)
- φυλάσσεσθε: present middle imperative = "be on your guard/guard yourselves" — the ongoing vigilance required
- ἵνα μή: "so that…not" — purpose clause with subjunctive
- συναπαχθέντες: aorist passive participle of συναπάγω = "having been carried away together with" — the image is of being swept along in the current of error; compound: σύν (together) + ἀπό (away) + ἄγω (to lead)
- τῇ τῶν ἀθέσμων πλάνῃ: "by the error of the unprincipled/lawless ones" — ἄθεσμος = "without established law/norm"; same term as 2:7 (of the Sodomites); the false teachers are linked lexically to Sodom
- στηριγμοῦ [NT-HAPAX]: "steadfastness/firm standing" — only NT occurrence; from the same root as ἀστήρικτοι ("unstable," v.16; 2:14); the believers are to hold their firm standing, which is the opposite of the unstable ones who distort
- ἐκπέσητε: aorist subjunctive of ἐκπίπτω = "fall from/fall out of" — the danger is real: the believer can fall from an established position

**v.18**
but grow in the grace and knowledge of our Lord and Savior Jesus Christ. To him be the glory both now and to the day of eternity. Amen.

*Notes:*
- αὐξάνετε δέ: "but grow" — present imperative; the command is ongoing; the δέ marks the positive counterpart to the negative warning of v.17 (guard yourselves / grow)
- ἐν χάριτι καὶ γνώσει: "in the grace and knowledge" — the two spheres of growth; γνώσις here (not ἐπίγνωσις as in 1:2, 3, 8; 2:20); the variation may or may not be significant [INTERP-RISK: minor — γνώσις vs. ἐπίγνωσις; some argue the terms are interchangeable in 2 Peter, others that ἐπίγνωσις carries a stronger "full knowledge" force; rendered literally]
- τοῦ Κυρίου ἡμῶν καὶ Σωτῆρος Ἰησοῦ Χριστοῦ: "of our Lord and Savior Jesus Christ" — the full christological title; a single article governs both Κυρίου and Σωτῆρος (Granville Sharp construction), identifying one person
- αὐτῷ ἡ δόξα: "to him be the glory" — doxological formula; the letter ends with a doxology (unusual for a Petrine letter — 1 Peter has a doxology at 4:11 but ends with greetings at 5:14)
- καὶ νῦν καὶ εἰς ἡμέραν αἰῶνος: "both now and to the day of eternity" — the temporal scope spans from present to the eschatological day; αἰῶνος = "of eternity/of the age" [INTERP-RISK: "day of eternity" is an unusual phrase — ἡμέρα αἰῶνος occurs only here in the NT; it may mean "the eternal day" or "the day that belongs to eternity"; rendered literally]
- ἀμήν: "Amen" — the closing affirmation
