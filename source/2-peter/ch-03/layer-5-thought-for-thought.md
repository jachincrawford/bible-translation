**v.1**
Dear friends, this is now the second letter I am writing to you. In both of them I have tried to stir up your sincere minds by reminding you of what is true.

*Rendering Notes (S4 → S5):*
- [↔] "pure mind" → "sincere minds" — εἰλικρινῆ διάνοιαν rendered as "sincere minds" for natural English; "sincere" captures the sense of unmixed, genuine thinking more accessibly than "pure" in this context
- [↔] "I am stirring up your mind by way of reminder" → "I have tried to stir up your sincere minds by reminding you of what is true" — the purpose clause is rendered more naturally; "what is true" makes explicit the content of the reminder without adding doctrine
- [↔] "Beloved" → "Dear friends" — ἀγαπητοί rendered in the NIV tradition; "Dear friends" is immediately warm and accessible without the archaic register of "Beloved"

---

**v.2**
I want you to remember the words spoken long ago by the holy prophets, and the command given by our Lord and Savior through your apostles.

*Rendering Notes (S4 → S5):*
- [↔] "the words previously spoken by the holy prophets" → "the words spoken long ago by the holy prophets" — "long ago" is more natural than "previously" for προειρημένων and situates the prophets historically for a reader without biblical background
- [↔] "the commandment of the Lord and Savior through your apostles" → "the command given by our Lord and Savior through your apostles" — "command given" makes the agency explicit; "our" instead of "the" makes the relational connection personal
- [↔] Two-source authority structure preserved — prophets (OT) and apostles (NT) are presented as complementary witnesses; remarkable claim of apostolic authority alongside Scripture

---

**v.3**
Above all, understand this: in the last days people will come and make fun of you, following their own corrupt desires.

*Rendering Notes (S4 → S5):*
- [↔] "scoffers will come with their scoffing" → "people will come and make fun of you" — the cognate phrase ἐμπαῖκται ἐμπαιγμονῇ is rendered naturally; "make fun of you" is immediately accessible where "scoffers scoffing" feels redundant in English; the personal target ("you") is made explicit
- [↔] "walking according to their own lusts" → "following their own corrupt desires" — "following" is more natural than "walking according to"; "corrupt desires" renders ἐπιθυμίας without the baggage of "lusts" for a contemporary reader
- [↔] "Above all, understand this" — γινώσκοντες τοῦτο πρῶτον rendered as an imperative for natural English; the priority marker ("above all") is preserved

---

**v.4**
They will say, "Where is this 'coming' he promised? Ever since our ancestors died, everything goes on just as it has since the beginning of creation."

*Rendering Notes (S4 → S5):*
- [↔] "the fathers fell asleep" → "our ancestors died" — "fell asleep" (ἐκοιμήθησαν) is a euphemism that may confuse readers unfamiliar with the idiom; "died" is direct and clear; "ancestors" renders πατέρες without requiring knowledge of patriarchal terminology
- [↔] "Where is the promise of his coming?" → "Where is this 'coming' he promised?" — restructured to sound like natural mockery; the scare quotes around "coming" capture the scoffers' dismissive tone
- [↔] "all things continue as they were from the beginning of creation" → "everything goes on just as it has since the beginning of creation" — "everything goes on" is more colloquial and captures the scoffers' bored dismissiveness; the uniformitarian argument is preserved in accessible language

---

**v.5**
They deliberately forget that long ago, by God's word, the heavens came into being and the earth was formed out of water and through water.

*Rendering Notes (S4 → S5):*
- [↔] "this escapes them willfully" → "They deliberately forget" — λανθάνει αὐτοὺς τοῦτο θέλοντας rendered as active, intentional ignorance; "deliberately forget" is immediately clear — this is not innocent confusion but willful suppression of evidence
- [↔] "by the word of God" → "by God's word" — possessive form is more natural in English while preserving the agency of the divine λόγος
- [↔] "the earth was constituted out of water and through water" → "the earth was formed out of water and through water" — "formed" is more accessible than "constituted" for συνεστῶσα; the ἐξ ὕδατος καὶ δι᾽ ὕδατος phrase is preserved exactly, pointing to Genesis 1's creation account where water is the primordial medium

---

**v.6**
Through these waters the world of that time was deluged and destroyed.

*Rendering Notes (S4 → S5):*
- [↔] "the world at that time was destroyed, being flooded with water" → "Through these waters the world of that time was deluged and destroyed" — "through these waters" connects back to the water-creation of v.5, showing the irony: the same element that formed the world also destroyed it
- [↔] "deluged" retained — κατακλυσθείς is the root of English "cataclysm"; "deluged" preserves the catastrophic scale while remaining accessible
- [↔] Concise single sentence — the brevity matches the swiftness of the destruction; no expansion needed

---

**v.7**
By that same word, the present heavens and earth are being reserved for fire, kept for the day of judgment and destruction of the ungodly.

*Rendering Notes (S4 → S5):*
- [↔] "the now heavens and the earth" → "the present heavens and earth" — "present" is more natural than "now" as an adjective modifying nouns; the νῦν is temporal-contrastive (this present arrangement vs. the old one)
- [↔] "stored up for fire" → "being reserved for fire" — τεθησαυρισμένοι rendered as ongoing state; "reserved" implies deliberate holding for a purpose, which captures the perfect passive force
- [↔] "the day of judgment and destruction of ungodly men" → "the day of judgment and destruction of the ungodly" — "the ungodly" is slightly more natural than "ungodly men" and avoids unnecessary gendered language; the water-fire-judgment progression (vv.5-7) now stands complete

---

**v.8**
But do not forget this one thing, dear friends: with the Lord one day is like a thousand years, and a thousand years are like one day.

*Rendering Notes (S4 → S5):*
- [↔] "let this one thing not escape you" → "do not forget this one thing" — the λανθανέτω echo of v.5 is preserved in English through the "forget" link (the scoffers deliberately forget; the believers must not forget); the imperative is direct and natural
- [↔] "Beloved" → "dear friends" — consistent with v.1 rendering of ἀγαπητοί
- [↔] Psalm 90:4 allusion preserved exactly — the chiastic structure (one day → thousand years / thousand years → one day) is maintained; this is not saying God cannot tell time but that divine patience operates on a fundamentally different scale than human expectation

---

**v.9**
The Lord is not slow in keeping his promise, as some people understand slowness. Instead, he is patient with you, not wanting anyone to perish but wanting everyone to come to repentance.

*Rendering Notes (S4 → S5):*
- [↔] "The Lord does not delay concerning the promise" → "The Lord is not slow in keeping his promise" — follows NIV; "keeping his promise" is more concrete and relational than "concerning the promise"; the defense of God's faithfulness is foregrounded
- [↔] "as some count slowness" → "as some people understand slowness" — "understand" is slightly more natural than "count" for ἡγέομαι in this context; the scoffers' misreading of patience as inaction is gently corrected
- [↔] "not wishing any to perish" → "not wanting anyone to perish" — "wanting" is more natural than "wishing" for contemporary English; the universalist scope of βουλόμενος (directed will/desire) is preserved without theological expansion

---

**v.10**
But the day of the Lord will come like a thief. The heavens will disappear with a rushing roar, the elements will be destroyed by intense heat, and the earth and everything done in it will be laid bare.

*Rendering Notes (S4 → S5):*
- [↔] "with a rushing roar" — ῥοιζηδόν is an onomatopoeic hapax legomenon; "rushing roar" captures both the sound and the speed; the word itself imitates the noise it describes
- [↔] "will be laid bare" — the εὑρεθήσεται textual crux is rendered following the reading that takes the sense as "found/exposed/laid bare" rather than "burned up" (κατακαήσεται); the earth and its works are exposed before divine judgment, not merely annihilated; this follows the best-attested manuscript reading
- [↔] "the elements will be dissolved with intense heat" → "the elements will be destroyed by intense heat" — "destroyed" is more accessible than "dissolved" for λυθήσεται in this apocalyptic context; στοιχεῖα likely refers to the fundamental components of the physical universe

---

**v.11**
Since everything will be destroyed in this way, what kind of people ought you to be? You ought to live holy and godly lives

*Rendering Notes (S4 → S5):*
- [↔] "all these things being dissolved" → "everything will be destroyed in this way" — future passive rendered actively for natural English; "in this way" connects back to the cosmic dissolution of v.10
- [↔] "what sort of people must you be" → "what kind of people ought you to be" — "ought" is slightly softer than "must" while preserving the moral imperative of δεῖ; the rhetorical question demands self-examination
- [↔] "in holy conduct and godliness" → "live holy and godly lives" — the abstract nouns are rendered as a lifestyle description; "lives" makes the application concrete and practical

---

**v.12**
as you look forward to the day of God and work to bring it closer. On that day the heavens will be set on fire and destroyed, and the elements will melt in intense heat.

*Rendering Notes (S4 → S5):*
- [↔] "hastening the coming of the day of God" → "work to bring it closer" — σπεύδοντας is the crux; it can mean either "eagerly awaiting" or "hastening/speeding up"; the rendering "work to bring it closer" captures the active sense (that holy living actually accelerates the consummation) in natural English without the awkwardness of "hastening"
- [↔] "the heavens, being set on fire, will be dissolved" → "the heavens will be set on fire and destroyed" — the participial chain is rendered as coordinate verbs for natural English rhythm
- [↔] "the elements will melt with intense heat" — τήκεται rendered as "melt" (the literal sense); the image of cosmic melting intensifies the fire imagery from v.10

---

**v.13**
But in keeping with his promise, we look forward to new heavens and a new earth, where righteousness is at home.

*Rendering Notes (S4 → S5):*
- [↔] "according to his promise" → "in keeping with his promise" — more natural English idiom; the promise-fulfillment theme running through the chapter (v.4: "where is the promise?"; v.9: "not slow about his promise"; v.13: "in keeping with his promise") reaches its climax here
- [↔] "in which righteousness dwells" → "where righteousness is at home" — κατοικεῖ means "to settle down, to make one's home"; "is at home" captures the permanent-residence sense more vividly than "dwells," which can feel archaic; righteousness will not be a visitor but a permanent resident
- [↔] "new heavens and a new earth" — καινούς (qualitatively new, renewed) is rendered simply as "new"; the Isaiah 65:17 / 66:22 background is present but not explained in the translation itself

---

**v.14**
So then, dear friends, since you are looking forward to these things, make every effort to be found spotless, blameless, and at peace with him.

*Rendering Notes (S4 → S5):*
- [↔] "be diligent to be found" → "make every effort to be found" — σπουδάσατε rendered with the NIV idiom "make every effort," which echoes 2 Peter 1:5 and creates an inclusio with the opening chapter
- [↔] "without spot and blemish" → "spotless, blameless" — ἄσπιλοι καὶ ἀμώμητοι rendered as a pair of accessible adjectives; note the deliberate contrast with the false teachers who are "blots and blemishes" (2:13) — what they are, the believers must not be
- [↔] "in peace" → "at peace with him" — "with him" makes the relational dimension explicit; εἰρήνη here is not merely tranquility but right-standing before God

---

**v.15**
Consider that our Lord's patience means salvation — just as our dear brother Paul also wrote to you with the wisdom that God gave him.

*Rendering Notes (S4 → S5):*
- [↔] "regard the patience of our Lord as salvation" → "Consider that our Lord's patience means salvation" — "means salvation" is a more natural English construction than "regard…as"; the theological point is preserved: every day of apparent delay is a day of offered rescue
- [↔] "our beloved brother Paul" → "our dear brother Paul" — ἀγαπητός rendered consistently with the translation's register; "dear" is warm without being archaic
- [↔] "according to the wisdom given to him" → "with the wisdom that God gave him" — "God" is made explicit as the source of wisdom; this is a remarkable inter-apostolic acknowledgment: Peter affirms Paul's letters as wisdom-given, not merely personal opinion; this verse is one of the strongest internal witnesses to apostolic awareness of a developing canon

---

**v.16**
He writes the same way in all his letters, speaking in them about these matters. Some parts of his letters are hard to understand, and ignorant and unstable people distort them, as they do the other Scriptures, to their own destruction.

*Rendering Notes (S4 → S5):*
- [↔] "in which are some things hard to understand" → "Some parts of his letters are hard to understand" — restructured for natural English; the concessive honesty is preserved — Peter does not pretend Paul is always easy to read
- [↔] "the untaught and unstable" → "ignorant and unstable people" — ἀμαθεῖς rendered as "ignorant" (without learning), not merely "uninstructed"; the pairing with "unstable" (ἀστήρικτοι) describes people who lack both knowledge and rootedness
- [↔] "twist" → "distort" — στρεβλοῦσιν means to twist on the rack, to torture; "distort" captures the violent wrenching of meaning; "as they do the other Scriptures" (ὡς καὶ τὰς λοιπὰς γραφάς) — this clause implicitly places Paul's letters alongside "the other Scriptures," a striking canonical claim

---

**v.17**
Therefore, dear friends, since you already know this, be on your guard so that you are not carried away by the error of lawless people and lose your firm footing.

*Rendering Notes (S4 → S5):*
- [↔] "fall from your own steadfastness" → "lose your firm footing" — στηριγμοῦ rendered as "firm footing" rather than "steadfastness" or "secure position"; "firm footing" is a concrete, physical image that captures both stability and the danger of slipping; it connects to ἀστήρικτοι ("unstable") in v.16 by contrast — what the ignorant lack, the readers must not lose
- [↔] "carried away by the error of unprincipled men" → "carried away by the error of lawless people" — συναπαχθέντες is vivid: swept along as by a current; "lawless" renders ἀθέσμων (those who reject established boundaries)
- [↔] "knowing beforehand" → "since you already know this" — προγινώσκοντες rendered as a causal clause; the forewarning gives responsibility: you cannot claim ignorance

---

**v.18**
Instead, grow in the grace and knowledge of our Lord and Savior Jesus Christ. To him be glory both now and to the day of eternity. Amen.

*Rendering Notes (S4 → S5):*
- [↔] "grow in grace and in the knowledge" → "grow in the grace and knowledge" — the single article τῇ governing both χάριτι and γνώσει suggests these are a unified pair, not two separate categories; grace and knowledge of Christ grow together
- [↔] "to the day of eternity" — εἰς ἡμέραν αἰῶνος is an unusual phrase (only here in the NT); "to the day of eternity" preserves the paradox — a "day" that is also "eternity"; the letter that began with scoffers asking about "the day" ends with the only day that matters
- [↔] "Amen" retained — the doxological conclusion mirrors 1 Peter 5:11 and Jude 25; the letter closes not with fear of the coming fire but with worship of the coming Lord
