**v.1**
This is already the second letter I am writing to you, beloved, in which I stir up your sincere mind by way of reminder,

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: ταύτην ἤδη δευτέραν ἐπιστολήν → "this is already the second letter"; the adverb ἤδη ("already") conveys that the act of writing a second time is itself significant — the repetition is purposeful, not incidental]
- [TC: εἰλικρινῆ διάνοιαν → "sincere mind"; εἰλικρινής = tested by sunlight and found pure/genuine; the compound image (sun + judging) conveys transparency; "sincere" captures the moral purity; "mind" renders διάνοια = the faculty of thought/understanding]
- [TC: ἐν ὑπομνήσει → "by way of reminder"; the dative of means/manner; the letter's function is recollection, not novelty; same motif as 1:12–15]

**v.2**
to remember the words spoken beforehand by the holy prophets, and the commandment of the Lord and Savior given through your apostles.

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: μνησθῆναι → "to remember"; aorist passive infinitive expressing purpose — the stirring up (v.1) aims at remembrance]
- [TC: τῶν προειρημένων ῥημάτων → "the words spoken beforehand"; the perfect passive participle of προλέγω indicates the prophetic words are already on record and remain authoritative; "beforehand" preserves the temporal priority of the prophetic word]
- [TC: τῆς τῶν ἀποστόλων ὑμῶν ἐντολῆς τοῦ Κυρίου καὶ Σωτῆρος → "the commandment of the Lord and Savior given through your apostles"; the genitive chain requires careful unpacking: the commandment belongs to the Lord and Savior, mediated through the apostles; "given through" makes the chain of transmission explicit without adding to the Greek]

**v.3**
Know this above all: that in the last days scoffers will come, mocking as they go, walking according to their own desires,

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: γινώσκοντες τοῦτο πρῶτον → "know this above all"; the participle functions as an imperatival exhortation; πρῶτον = "first/above all" marks this as the foundational warning of the chapter]
- [TC: ἐμπαῖκται ἐν ἐμπαιγμονῇ → "scoffers...mocking as they go"; ἐμπαιγμονή [NT-HAPAX] is the cognate noun to ἐμπαίζω; the construction is intensifying — scoffers characterized by their scoffing; "mocking as they go" captures the habitual, defining nature of their mockery without the awkwardness of "with mockery"]
- [TC: κατὰ τὰς ἰδίας ἐπιθυμίας αὐτῶν πορευόμενοι → "walking according to their own desires"; the double possessive (ἰδίας + αὐτῶν) is emphatic — their desires are emphatically their own, not from God]

**v.4**
and saying, "Where is the promise of his coming? For ever since the fathers fell asleep, all things have continued just as they were from the beginning of creation."

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: ποῦ ἐστιν ἡ ἐπαγγελία τῆς παρουσίας αὐτοῦ → "where is the promise of his coming"; the rhetorical question is dismissive, not inquiring; παρουσία = arrival/presence, the standard term for Christ's return]
- [TC: οἱ πατέρες → "the fathers"; the referent is debated — OT patriarchs or the first generation of Christians; "the fathers" preserved without resolution; the scoffers' argument requires that sufficient time has elapsed to falsify the promise]
- [TC: πάντα οὕτως διαμένει ἀπ᾽ ἀρχῆς κτίσεως → "all things have continued just as they were from the beginning of creation"; the scoffers assert cosmic uniformity — nothing has changed, therefore nothing will change; the present tense διαμένει conveys ongoing, unbroken continuity]

---

**v.5**
For they deliberately overlook this: that the heavens existed long ago, and the earth was formed out of water and through water by the word of God,

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: λανθάνει αὐτοὺς τοῦτο θέλοντας → "they deliberately overlook this"; the participle θέλοντας ("willingly/deliberately") is the crux — the ignorance is volitional; the scoffers choose not to know; "deliberately overlook" captures both the ignorance (λανθάνει) and the volition (θέλοντας)]
- [TC: οὐρανοὶ ἦσαν ἔκπαλαι → "the heavens existed long ago"; ἔκπαλαι = "from of old/long ago," a rare adverb (also at 2:3); the antiquity of the heavens refutes the scoffers' claim of cosmic stasis — the world has a history of divine intervention]
- [TC: γῆ ἐξ ὕδατος καὶ δι᾽ ὕδατος συνεστῶσα → "the earth was formed out of water and through water"; ἐξ = origin, δι᾽ = instrumentality; the double preposition emphasizes water's dual cosmological role in the earth's formation (Gen 1:2, 6–10); συνεστῶσα (perfect participle of συνίστημι) = constituted and held in coherent existence]
- [TC: τῷ τοῦ Θεοῦ λόγῳ → "by the word of God"; dative of instrument — the divine word is the active agent; this sets up the parallel in v.7 ("by the same word")]

---

**v.6**
through which the world at that time, having been deluged with water, was destroyed.

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: δι᾽ ὧν → "through which"; the plural relative pronoun most likely refers to the waters mentioned in v.5; the same element that formed the world also destroyed it — water is both creative and destructive by divine command]
- [TC: ὁ τότε κόσμος → "the world at that time"; τότε sets the destroyed world apart from the present world (v.7); the distinction between worlds is load-bearing for the argument]
- [TC: ὕδατι κατακλυσθείς → "having been deluged with water"; κατακλύζω = to inundate/flood; the aorist passive participle marks the flooding as the means of destruction; the cognate noun κατακλυσμός = the Flood (Gen 6–8)]
- [TC: ἀπώλετο → "was destroyed"; second aorist middle of ἀπόλλυμι; definitive, total destruction — the pre-flood world is gone; this establishes the precedent: God has destroyed a world before and can do so again]

---

**v.7**
But the present heavens and earth, by the same word, have been stored up for fire, being kept for the day of judgment and destruction of ungodly people.

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: οἱ νῦν οὐρανοὶ καὶ ἡ γῆ → "the present heavens and earth"; νῦν = "now/present"; the contrast with the pre-flood world (v.6) is explicit — this world too is under divine sovereignty]
- [TC: τῷ αὐτῷ λόγῳ → "by the same word"; the divine word that created (v.5) and destroyed (v.6) now preserves the present cosmos for its appointed end; the continuity of divine agency is the theological point]
- [TC: τεθησαυρισμένοι εἰσίν πυρί → "have been stored up for fire"; perfect passive periphrastic of θησαυρίζω; the present cosmos is a divine storehouse awaiting its appointed fire; πυρί (dative) = "for fire" — fire is the future agent, as water was the past agent]
- [TC: τηρούμενοι → "being kept"; present passive participle — the τηρέω motif continues from 2:4, 9, 17; divine custody for future judgment; the keeping is both preservation and reservation]
- [TC: ἀσεβῶν ἀνθρώπων → "ungodly people"; the ἀσεβ- word group recurs throughout 2 Peter (2:5, 6; 3:7); the judgment targets the ungodly specifically]

**v.8**
But do not let this one thing escape you, beloved: that with the Lord one day is like a thousand years, and a thousand years like one day.

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: ἓν τοῦτο μὴ λανθανέτω ὑμᾶς → "do not let this one thing escape you"; the verb λανθάνω echoes v.5 (where the same verb describes the scoffers' willful ignorance); the believers must not share the scoffers' blindness; the imperative is emphatic — "this one thing"]
- [TC: μία ἡμέρα παρὰ Κυρίῳ ὡς χίλια ἔτη → "one day with the Lord is like a thousand years"; allusion to Ps 90:4 (LXX 89:4); the chiastic structure (day → thousand years → thousand years → day) is preserved; ὡς = "like/as" — simile, not equation; divine temporality is qualitatively different from human reckoning, not merely quantitatively larger]
- [TC: ἀγαπητοί → "beloved"; the third of four occurrences in ch. 3 (vv.1, 8, 14, 17); the pastoral address marks the shift from refuting the scoffers (vv.3–7) to reassuring the faithful (vv.8–9)]

**v.9**
The Lord does not delay his promise, as some reckon delay, but he is patient toward you, not willing that any should perish but that all should reach repentance.

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: οὐ βραδύνει Κύριος τῆς ἐπαγγελίας → "the Lord does not delay his promise"; βραδύνω = to be slow, to tarry; the genitive τῆς ἐπαγγελίας is genitive of reference/respect; the scoffers' charge (v.4) is directly answered]
- [TC: ὥς τινες βραδύτητα ἡγοῦνται → "as some reckon delay"; τινες = "some" — a restrained reference to the scoffers; their framework for evaluating delay is being rejected]
- [TC: μακροθυμεῖ εἰς ὑμᾶς → "he is patient toward you"; μακροθυμέω = to be long-tempered, to exercise patience; the divine "delay" is reframed as divine patience; εἰς ὑμᾶς = "toward you" — the patience is directed at the recipients, though the scope may extend beyond them]
- [TC: μὴ βουλόμενός τινας ἀπολέσθαι ἀλλὰ πάντας εἰς μετάνοιαν χωρῆσαι → "not willing that any should perish but that all should reach repentance"; βούλομαι = to will, to purpose; the scope of τινας ("any") and πάντας ("all") — whether universal or limited to the addressees — is a longstanding exegetical question; the Greek is rendered without restricting the scope; χωρέω = to make room, to advance toward — "reach" captures the directional force]

**v.10**
But the day of the Lord will come like a thief, in which the heavens will pass away with a rushing roar, and the elements will be dissolved with burning heat, and the earth and the works in it will be laid bare.

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: ὡς κλέπτης → "like a thief"; the simile emphasizes sudden, unexpected arrival; the point is timing, not stealth; cf. 1 Thess 5:2; Matt 24:43]
- [TC: ῥοιζηδόν → "with a rushing roar" [NT-HAPAX]; onomatopoeic adverb from ῥοῖζος = a rushing, whistling, or hissing sound; the dissolution is violent and audible; "with a rushing roar" captures both the speed (rushing) and the sound (roar) encoded in the Greek]
- [TC: στοιχεῖα → "elements"; the referent spans several possibilities in Hellenistic usage: (a) physical elements of matter, (b) celestial bodies, (c) elemental principles, (d) spiritual powers; the context (cosmic dissolution, fire, melting) favors a cosmological sense — either physical elements or heavenly bodies; "elements" is retained as the term that covers the range without foreclosing the referent]
- [TC: καυσούμενα λυθήσεται → "will be dissolved with burning heat"; the combination of present participle (καυσούμενα = being burned/scorched) with future passive (λυθήσεται = will be dissolved) conveys ongoing intense heat culminating in dissolution]
- [TC: εὑρεθήσεται → "will be laid bare"; this is the most difficult textual and interpretive crux in 2 Peter; the N1904 reading εὑρεθήσεται (future passive of εὑρίσκω = "will be found") is the lectio difficilior; the sense is that the earth and its works will be found out, exposed, laid bare before divine scrutiny — nothing will be hidden; "laid bare" renders the theological force of the finding/discovery; major variants: κατακαήσεται ("will be burned up," P72 and many minuscules), οὐχ εὑρεθήσεται ("will not be found," Sinaiticus corrector), ἀφανισθήσονται ("will vanish"); the N1904 reading is followed per project source text]

**v.11**
Since all these things are being dissolved in this way, what kind of people ought you to be — living in holy conduct and godliness,

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: τούτων οὕτως πάντων λυομένων → "since all these things are being dissolved in this way"; genitive absolute; the present passive participle λυομένων treats the dissolution as either in process or so certain as to be stated as present reality; the cosmological undoing grounds the ethical imperative]
- [TC: ποταποὺς δεῖ ὑπάρχειν ὑμᾶς → "what kind of people ought you to be"; ποταπός = "of what sort/kind"; the question is rhetorical, expecting an emphatic answer; the dash signals that the answer follows in the prepositional phrases]
- [TC: ἐν ἁγίαις ἀναστροφαῖς καὶ εὐσεβείαις → "in holy conduct and godliness"; the plurals are unusual — possibly indicating multiple expressions/instances of holy behavior and piety; "conduct" renders ἀναστροφή = manner of life; εὐσέβεια = reverent devotion/godliness; the pair encompasses both ethics and worship]

**v.12**
looking for and hastening the coming of the day of God, on account of which the heavens, being set ablaze, will be dissolved, and the elements will melt with burning heat!

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: προσδοκῶντας καὶ σπεύδοντας → "looking for and hastening"; σπεύδω is the exegetical crux: (a) transitive "hastening" = actively accelerating the day's arrival through repentance and holy living (cf. the rabbinic principle that Israel's repentance hastens redemption; Acts 3:19–20 may also support this), or (b) intransitive "eagerly desiring/earnestly awaiting"; the transitive reading is the more theologically distinctive and is grammatically defensible when σπεύδω takes an accusative object (τὴν παρουσίαν); "hastening" is adopted as the primary rendering]
- [TC: τὴν παρουσίαν τῆς τοῦ Θεοῦ ἡμέρας → "the coming of the day of God"; note the shift from "day of the Lord" (v.10) to "day of God"; the terms may be synonymous, or the shift may indicate a distinct eschatological emphasis — the day when God's purposes are fully realized]
- [TC: πυρούμενοι λυθήσονται → "being set ablaze, will be dissolved"; πυρόω = to set on fire/ignite; the heavens are not merely warming but burning]
- [TC: τήκεται → "will melt"; present passive of τήκω = to melt/dissolve; a different verb from λύω (vv.10, 11), adding the specific image of melting under extreme heat — metals, wax, or ice liquefying]
- [TC: στοιχεῖα → "elements"; same term as v.10; same interpretive range; the repetition underscores the totality of cosmic dissolution]

**v.13**
But according to his promise, we are looking for new heavens and a new earth, in which righteousness is at home.

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: κατὰ τὸ ἐπάγγελμα αὐτοῦ → "according to his promise"; ἐπάγγελμα = the promise itself (the noun, not the act of promising); the referent is Isa 65:17 and 66:22; the promise grounds the hope]
- [TC: καινοὺς οὐρανοὺς καὶ γῆν καινήν → "new heavens and a new earth"; καινός = new in quality/character (renewed, fresh), not merely new in time (νέος); the emphatic positioning of καινούς before the noun in both phrases stresses the newness]
- [TC: δικαιοσύνη κατοικεῖ → "righteousness is at home"; κατοικέω = to dwell permanently, to settle, to be at home (distinct from παροικέω = to sojourn as a foreigner); "is at home" captures the permanence and belonging that κατοικέω conveys — righteousness will not be a visitor but a permanent resident in the renewed creation]

**v.14**
Therefore, beloved, since you are looking for these things, make every effort to be found by him without spot and without blemish, at peace.

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: σπουδάσατε → "make every effort"; aorist imperative of σπουδάζω = to be diligent, to exert oneself; cf. 1:5 (same verb); the urgency of the imperative is load-bearing — this is not a suggestion but a command]
- [TC: ἄσπιλοι καὶ ἀμώμητοι → "without spot and without blemish"; the pair deliberately contrasts with 2:13 (σπίλοι καὶ μῶμοι = "stains and blemishes" of the false teachers); the believers are to be the precise moral antithesis; ἄσπιλος = unspotted; ἀμώμητος = without blemish/blame]
- [TC: εὑρεθῆναι αὐτῷ → "to be found by him"; the verb εὑρίσκω echoes v.10 (εὑρεθήσεται); as the earth will be "found/laid bare" at judgment, so the believers should be "found" in a state of purity — the verbal echo links cosmic and personal accountability]
- [TC: ἐν εἰρήνῃ → "at peace"; the closing note of the exhortation; peace as the condition in which the believer awaits the parousia — in contrast to the turmoil of the false teachers and the scoffers]

**v.15**
And consider the patience of our Lord as salvation — just as also our beloved brother Paul, according to the wisdom given to him, wrote to you,

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: τὴν τοῦ Κυρίου ἡμῶν μακροθυμίαν σωτηρίαν ἡγεῖσθε → "consider the patience of our Lord as salvation"; the imperative ἡγεῖσθε = consider/regard; μακροθυμία (cf. v.9 μακροθυμεῖ) is explicitly identified as σωτηρία — the delay is not negligence but saving patience; the reframing is the theological center of the argument]
- [TC: ὁ ἀγαπητὸς ἡμῶν ἀδελφὸς Παῦλος → "our beloved brother Paul"; ἀγαπητός = beloved, the same term used for the addressees (vv.1, 8, 14, 17); Paul is placed within the community of mutual affection; "brother" indicates collegial, not hierarchical, relationship]
- [TC: κατὰ τὴν δοθεῖσαν αὐτῷ σοφίαν → "according to the wisdom given to him"; the passive δοθεῖσαν (aorist passive participle of δίδωμι) points to divine endowment — Paul's wisdom is a gift, not merely acquired learning]
- [TC: ἔγραψεν ὑμῖν → "wrote to you"; aorist — a specific act of writing to the recipients; the particular Pauline letter is not identified from the Greek alone]

**v.16**
as also in all his letters, speaking in them about these things — among which are some things hard to understand, which the untaught and unstable twist, as they do the rest of the Scriptures, to their own destruction.

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: ὡς καὶ ἐν πάσαις ἐπιστολαῖς → "as also in all his letters"; the phrase presupposes knowledge of a collection of Pauline letters; πάσαις ("all") may mean "all his known letters" rather than implying a complete fixed corpus; the scope is rendered literally without specifying what "all" encompasses]
- [TC: δυσνόητα → "hard to understand" [NT-HAPAX]; compound adjective: δυσ- (difficult, as in English "dys-") + νοέω (to perceive with the mind); the acknowledgment of genuine difficulty in Paul's writings is remarkable and candid]
- [TC: ἀμαθεῖς → "untaught" [NT-HAPAX]; from ἀ- (privative) + μανθάνω (to learn); those who have not undergone proper instruction; "untaught" preserves the etymological force — these are people who lack training, not merely intelligence]
- [TC: ἀστήρικτοι → "unstable"; same adjective as 2:14; from ἀ- + στηρίζω (to establish firmly); those who lack a firm foundation; the verbal link to στηριγμοῦ (v.17) is deliberate]
- [TC: στρεβλοῦσιν → "twist" [NT-HAPAX]; from στρεβλόω, originally "to twist on the rack" (an instrument of torture); the image is of wrenching a text out of its natural shape, torturing its meaning; "twist" captures the distortion; "distort" is equally valid]
- [TC: τὰς λοιπὰς γραφάς → "the rest of the Scriptures"; λοιπάς = "the rest/remaining"; the construction most naturally implies that Paul's letters belong to the same category as "the Scriptures" (γραφαί) — they twist Paul's letters as they do the rest of the Scriptures; this is load-bearing for the canonical status of Pauline writings within the text's own framework]
- [TC: πρὸς τὴν ἰδίαν αὐτῶν ἀπώλειαν → "to their own destruction"; the distortion rebounds upon the distorters; the double possessive (ἰδίαν + αὐτῶν) is emphatic]

**v.17**
You then, beloved — since you know this in advance — guard yourselves, so that you are not carried away by the error of lawless people and fall from your own firm standing,

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: ὑμεῖς οὖν ἀγαπητοί → "you then, beloved"; emphatic pronoun ὑμεῖς + the fourth and final ἀγαπητοί in ch. 3; the direct address is at maximum pastoral intensity]
- [TC: προγινώσκοντες → "since you know this in advance"; the foreknowledge of the danger is the ground for vigilance — the letter itself constitutes the advance warning; the participle is causal]
- [TC: φυλάσσεσθε → "guard yourselves"; present middle imperative — ongoing, self-directed vigilance; the middle voice places responsibility on the believers themselves]
- [TC: συναπαχθέντες → "carried away"; aorist passive participle of συναπάγω = to carry away together with, to sweep along; the image is of being caught in a current; σύν (together) + ἀπό (away) + ἄγω (to lead) — swept away together with the errorists]
- [TC: τῶν ἀθέσμων πλάνῃ → "the error of lawless people"; ἄθεσμος = without established law/norm; same term as 2:7 (of the Sodomites); the lexical link binds the present danger to the historic judgment]
- [TC: στηριγμοῦ → "firm standing" [NT-HAPAX]; from στηρίζω = to set firmly, to establish; the noun occurs only here in the NT; it deliberately contrasts with ἀστήρικτοι ("unstable," v.16; 2:14) — the believers possess the very stability that the distorters lack; "firm standing" preserves the concrete, positional metaphor]
- [TC: ἐκπέσητε → "fall from"; aorist subjunctive of ἐκπίπτω; the danger is real and specified — one can fall from an established position of stability; the prefix ἐκ- ("out of/from") marks departure from a held position]

**v.18**
but grow in the grace and knowledge of our Lord and Savior Jesus Christ. To him be glory both now and to the day of eternity. Amen.

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: αὐξάνετε δέ → "but grow"; present imperative — the command is ongoing and open-ended; the δέ marks the positive counterpart to the negative warning of v.17; the letter's final imperative is not defensive (guard) but generative (grow)]
- [TC: ἐν χάριτι καὶ γνώσει → "in the grace and knowledge"; the two spheres of growth; γνώσις here (not ἐπίγνωσις as in 1:2, 3, 8; 2:20); whether the distinction is significant or merely stylistic is debated; both grace and knowledge belong to Christ (genitive τοῦ Κυρίου)]
- [TC: τοῦ Κυρίου ἡμῶν καὶ Σωτῆρος Ἰησοῦ Χριστοῦ → "of our Lord and Savior Jesus Christ"; the full christological title; a single article governs Κυρίου and Σωτῆρος (Granville Sharp construction), identifying one person — Jesus Christ is both Lord and Savior]
- [TC: αὐτῷ ἡ δόξα → "to him be glory"; doxological formula; the letter ends not with greetings but with worship — a doxology directed to Christ]
- [TC: καὶ νῦν καὶ εἰς ἡμέραν αἰῶνος → "both now and to the day of eternity"; the temporal scope spans present to eschatological consummation; ἡμέρα αἰῶνος ("day of eternity") is unique in the NT — possibly "the day that inaugurates eternity" or "the eternal day"; the phrase gathers up all the "day" language of ch. 3 (day of judgment, v.7; day of the Lord, v.10; day of God, v.12) into a final, all-encompassing "day of eternity"]
- [TC: ἀμήν → "Amen"; the closing affirmation sealing the doxology and the entire letter]
