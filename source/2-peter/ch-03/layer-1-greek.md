## Editorial Conventions (Non-Variant Differences)

The following inter-tradition differences are **editorial** and are not recorded as variants below:
- **Capitalization** of divine titles (`Θεοῦ`/`θεοῦ`, `Κυρίου`/`κυρίου`, `Πνεύματος`/`πνεύματος`): N1904 capitalizes; SBLGNT/NA28/WH/RP print lower-case. Noted here once.
- **Punctuation** (comma / semicolon / em-dash placement): editors' reading aids; not textual.
- **Elision apostrophes** (`δι᾽` vs `διʼ` vs `δι'`): typographic.
- **Iota subscript** presence/absence: orthographic.

All other differences — word presence/absence, word choice, word order, spelling, and case changes — are tracked as variants.

### v.3

**Variants detected: 2**

**⟨Variant 1⟩** *Location: after `ἐπʼ` (in eschatological formula "in the last days")*
- SBLGNT, N1904, NA28, WH: `ἐσχάτων` (A-GPN — genitive plural "last [days]")
- RP: `ἐσχάτου` (A-GSM/N — genitive singular)
- Nature: case/number difference (plural → singular)
- Significance: minor — both yield "last of days"; the Byzantine singular follows a well-attested Koine idiom; the plural matches LXX phraseology

**⟨Variant 2⟩** *Location: prepositional phrase preceding `ἐμπαῖκται`*
