**v.1**
But false prophets also arose among the people, just as there will also be false teachers among you, who will secretly introduce destructive heresies, even denying the Master who bought them, bringing upon themselves swift destruction.

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: παρεισάξουσιν → "secretly introduce" per NASB 1995; the compound παρα+εις+άγω encodes the covert, alongside-infiltration; "bring in" (ESV) loses the stealth nuance; "smuggled in" (colloquial) too idiomatic for the register]
- [TC: δεσπότης → "Master" not "Lord"; δεσπότης = absolute owner/sovereign (the purchasing-owner construction ἀγοράσαντα αὐτοὺς δεσπότην makes the ownership explicit); κύριος is the standard "Lord" term — these are distinct]
- [TC: ταχινήν → "swift"; deliberate echo of 1:14 (ταχινή = Peter's own imminent death) — same adjective, now applied to the destroyers' fate; the echo is ironic and load-bearing]
- [TC: ψευδοδιδάσκαλοι → "false teachers" [NT-HAPAX]; compound hapax; standard rendering in all major versions]

---

**v.2**
And many will follow their licentious ways, because of whom the way of truth will be blasphemed.

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: ἐξακολουθήσουσιν → "will follow"; the ἐξ- prefix ("follow out/completely") marks thoroughgoing pursuit; "follow" is acceptable at this register, but the intensive force is noted — recurs at v.15 of Balaam]
- [TC: ἀσελγείαις → "licentious ways"; first of three occurrences in ch.2 (vv.2, 7, 18); the plural marks repeated, habitual acts rather than a single episode]
- [TC: ἡ ὁδὸς τῆς ἀληθείας → "the way of truth" — both articles preserved; "the way" is definite and singular, not one path among many]

---

**v.3**
And in their greed they will exploit you with fabricated words; their judgment, long operative, does not linger, and their destruction does not slumber.

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: πλαστοῖς λόγοις → "fabricated words" [NT-HAPAX: πλαστός]; "fabricated" captures the potter/sculptor imagery of deliberately shaped falsehood; NASB uses "false words," which is weaker]
- [TC: ἐμπορεύσονται → "exploit you"; the commercial trafficking verb (ἐμπορεύομαι) treats persons as tradeable commodity; "exploit" captures the predatory commercial force]
- [TC: κρίμα ἔκπαλαι οὐκ ἀργεῖ → "their judgment, long operative, does not linger" — personification of the legal sentence already standing from of old (ἔκπαλαι; rare, also at 3:5); the verdict is not idle/inactive, it moves]
- [TC: νυστάζει → "slumber"; vivid anthropomorphism — their destruction is not asleep at its post; same verb at Matt 25:5 (the virgins who slumbered)]

---

**v.4**
For if God did not spare angels when they sinned, but cast them into Tartarus and delivered them to pits of gloomy darkness, kept for judgment —

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: ταρταρώσας → "cast into Tartarus" rendered as "cast them into Tartarus" [NT-HAPAX: ταρταρόω]; NASB and ESV both render "cast into hell," which imports Gehenna/Hades associations; Tartarus is the Greco-Roman prison for the Titans — a cosmologically distinct referent; the hapax should not be collapsed to "hell" [INTERP-RISK: "hell" for ταρταρόω obscures the unique cosmological term]]
- [TC: σειροῖς ζόφου → "pits of gloomy darkness" following N1904 σειροῖς (pits/dungeons); the NA28/UBS5 variant σειραῖς = "chains of gloomy darkness" — distinct images; N1904 followed per source text]
- [TC: ζόφου → "gloomy darkness"; the Homeric/poetic darkness term (distinct from common σκότος); recurs in v.17]
- [TC: vv.4–9 form a single conditional sentence (protasis vv.4–8, apodosis v.9); verse ends with a dash to signal the open conditional chain]

---

**v.5**
and if he did not spare the ancient world, but preserved Noah, a herald of righteousness, as one of eight, when he brought a flood upon a world of ungodly people —

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: ὄγδοον → "as one of eight" (i.e., Noah among eight souls preserved; cf. 1 Pet 3:20); "as an eighth person" equally valid; the numeral emphasizes the extreme fewness of the preserved against universal judgment]
- [TC: κήρυκα δικαιοσύνης → "herald of righteousness"; κῆρυξ is the official public crier/proclaimer, not merely a "preacher" (which softens the official-proclamation force); the designation is extra-biblical tradition, not found in the Hebrew OT]
- [TC: κόσμῳ ἀσεβῶν → "a world of ungodly people"; genitive of characterization — the world defined by its ungodliness]

---

**v.6**
and if he condemned the cities of Sodom and Gomorrah to destruction by reducing them to ashes, making them an example of what is coming for the ungodly —

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: τεφρώσας → "reducing them to ashes" [NT-HAPAX: τεφρόω]; catastrophic finality — not merely burned but turned to ash; the aorist participle marks this as the means of the condemnation]
- [TC: καταστροφῇ → "to destruction"; the Greek root of the English "catastrophe"; dative of manner — condemned by way of overthrow/catastrophe]
- [TC: ὑπόδειγμα μελλόντων ἀσεβεῖν → "an example of what is coming for the ungodly"; the judgment is set as a standing didactic exhibit; "of what is coming" makes the proleptic force explicit]

---

**v.7**
and if he rescued righteous Lot, oppressed by the sensual conduct of unprincipled men —

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: καταπονούμενον → "oppressed"; the intensive κατα- prefix signals sustained, wearing-down pressure; NASB uses "oppressed," ESV "greatly distressed"; "oppressed" captures the ongoing attrition of the present participle]
- [TC: ἀθέσμων → "unprincipled men"; literally "those without established norm/law"; NASB "unprincipled men"; ESV "lawless people"]
- [TC: ἀσελγείᾳ ἀναστροφῆς → "sensual conduct"; the same ἀσέλγεια that marks the false teachers (v.2) links Sodom to the present danger; "conduct" renders ἀναστροφή = manner of life/behavior]

---

**v.8**
(for by what that righteous man saw and heard as he dwelt among them day after day, his righteous soul was tormented by their lawless deeds) —

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: βλέμματι καὶ ἀκοῇ → "by what he saw and heard"; βλέμμα [NT-HAPAX] = the act of sight; rendered as the content of sight and hearing rather than the abstract instruments, for natural English]
- [TC: ἡμέραν ἐξ ἡμέρας → "day after day"; the Greek idiom "day out of day" = unrelenting, repetitive duration; rendered with the standard English functional equivalent]
- [TC: ἐβασάνιζεν → "was tormented"; imperfect = repeated, ongoing anguish; βασανίζω originally = testing metals by touchstone, then torture; here the inner torment of a righteous soul amid moral squalor]
- [TC: ἐγκατοικῶν → "as he dwelt among them" [NT-HAPAX: ἐγκατοικέω]; the compound emphasizes embeddedness within a hostile environment]

---

**v.9**
the Lord knows how to rescue the godly from trial, and to keep the unrighteous under punishment for the day of judgment,

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: v.9 is the apodosis — the conclusion — of the extended conditional sentence begun in v.4; "the Lord knows how to" answers all the "if God did not spare" clauses]
- [TC: οἶδεν → "knows"; perfect with present force — the Lord's knowledge and competence are settled, not contingent]
- [TC: κολαζομένους → "under punishment"; present passive participle — the unrighteous are being punished during the custody, not merely awaiting future punishment; this is load-bearing for the doctrine of present divine retribution]
- [TC: ῥύεσθαι → "to rescue"; same root as ἐρύσατο (v.7) of Lot — the verbal link binds the historical rescue to the present divine principle]

---

**v.10**
and especially those who indulge the flesh in its corrupt desires and despise authority. Presumptuous, self-willed, they do not tremble when they blaspheme angelic majesties,

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: κυριότητος → "authority"; κυριότης = lordship/dominion; "authority" is the least interpretive gloss for what is likely a reference to angelic governing structures (cf. Jude 8)]
- [TC: δόξας → "angelic majesties" [INTERP-RISK: the referent is disputed — likely angelic beings or governing authorities; NASB uses "angelic majesties," ESV "glorious ones"; "glories" is the most literal but "angelic majesties" clarifies the likely referent without over-determining it]]
- [TC: τολμηταί → "presumptuous" [NT-HAPAX]; those who venture where they have no right; NASB "daring"; "presumptuous" carries the moral overtone of transgressing proper bounds]
- [TC: αὐθάδεις → "self-willed"; literally "pleasing-to-oneself"; only other NT occurrence at Titus 1:7]

---

**v.11**
whereas angels, though greater in might and power, do not bring a blasphemous judgment against them before the Lord.

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: ὅπου → "whereas"; adversative use — "whereas/where" introducing the contrasting conduct of angels; "whereas" makes the logical contrast with the false teachers explicit]
- [TC: ἰσχύϊ καὶ δυνάμει μείζονες → "greater in might and power"; the argument is a fortiori — if those who surpass the false teachers in raw power still restrain themselves, how much more should these men]
- [TC: φέρουσιν → "bring"; φέρω echo from 1:21 (φερόμενοι = "borne along" by the Spirit); here in the judicial sense of bringing/rendering a verdict; angels do not "bring" a blasphemous judgment even though they have far more cause and capacity]

---

**v.12**
But these, like irrational animals — creatures of instinct, born to be caught and destroyed — blaspheming in matters of which they are ignorant, will in their destruction also be destroyed,

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: ἄλογα ζῷα φυσικά → "irrational animals, creatures of instinct"; ἄλογος = without λόγος/reason; the irony is that these are teachers who speak, yet are without true reason; φυσικά = by nature, instinctually]
- [TC: φθοράν / φθορᾷ / φθαρήσονται → "destruction…destroyed" — triple φθορά paronomasia deliberately preserved; "born for destruction…will be destroyed in their destruction"]
- [TC: εἰς ἅλωσιν καὶ φθοράν → "to be caught and destroyed"; the hunting/capture image — born as prey animals, for the purpose of capture and destruction]

---

**v.13**
suffering wrong as the wage for wrongdoing. They count it a pleasure to revel in the daytime. They are stains and blemishes, reveling in their deceptions as they carouse with you,

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: ἀδικούμενοι μισθὸν ἀδικίας → "suffering wrong as the wage for wrongdoing"; the ironic wordplay on ἀδικία preserved; their "reward" is itself wrongdoing received; echo forward to v.15 (Balaam's "wage of unrighteousness")]
- [TC: ἀπάταις → "deceptions" following N1904; variant ἀγάπαις (P72, Vaticanus) = "love-feasts" — if the variant were followed, the communal meal setting would be explicit; N1904 text followed per project source]
- [TC: σπίλοι καὶ μῶμοι → "stains and blemishes"; vivid moral contamination metaphor; cf. Jude 12 (σπιλάδες); the pair contrasts with the spotless church (Eph 5:27)]
- [TC: τρυφήν → "revel"; daytime revelry inverts the social order — decent people work by day; "luxury" is lexically accurate but "revel" captures the self-indulgent excess in context]

---

**v.14**
having eyes full of adultery, never ceasing from sin, enticing unstable souls, having a heart trained in greed — accursed children;

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: μοιχαλίδος → "adultery"; Greek genitive is "of an adulteress" (concrete); "adultery" (abstract) is a legitimate English idiom — "eyes full of adultery" matches natural English while preserving the sense; cf. Matt 5:28]
- [TC: ἀκαταπαύστους → "never ceasing" [NT-HAPAX: ἀκατάπαυστος]; only NT occurrence; "insatiable" (NASB) and "insatiable for sin" are also defensible; "never ceasing" is more literal]
- [TC: καρδίαν γεγυμνασμένην πλεονεξίας → "a heart trained in greed"; the athletic-training metaphor applied to vice (γυμνάζω); perfect participle = the training is complete — a settled, accomplished mastery of greed]
- [TC: κατάρας τέκνα → "accursed children"; Semitic genitive of characterization; emphatic final position — the phrase lands as a verdict; NASB "children of a curse"]

---

**v.15**
Forsaking the right way, they have gone astray, having followed the way of Balaam, the son of Beor, who loved the wages of unrighteousness,

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: εὐθεῖαν ὁδόν → "the right way"; "straight way" (more literal) or "right way" — "right" captures both the moral and directional sense; the structural opposition of ways (right way / way of Balaam) is load-bearing]
- [TC: ἐξακολουθήσαντες → "having followed"; same verb as v.2 (future) — the followers of false teachers (v.2) and the false teachers following Balaam (v.15) are verbally bound]
- [TC: Βεώρ → "Beor" following N1904; variant Βοσόρ (Bosor) in many MSS; Beor is the Hebrew OT form (Num 22:5); N1904 followed per source text]
- [TC: μισθὸν ἀδικίας → "wages of unrighteousness"; exact phrase repeated from v.13 — Balaam is the archetype of the mercenary prophet who exploits spiritual authority for financial gain]

---

**v.16**
but he was rebuked for his own transgression — a speechless donkey, speaking with a human voice, restrained the madness of the prophet.

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: ὑποζύγιον ἄφωνον → "speechless donkey"; ὑποζύγιον = "under-yoke animal," the working beast (Num 22); ἄφωνος = voiceless/without speech — the rhetorical inversion is pointed: the voiceless one spoke]
- [TC: παραφρονίαν → "madness" [NT-HAPAX: παραφρονία]; only NT occurrence; the prophet's course = not merely error but outright insanity, rebuked by his own animal; "prophetic frenzy" or "delusion" are alternatives but "madness" is most direct]
- [TC: ἐκώλυσεν → "restrained"; the donkey actively prevented/stopped the seer's course; the devastating reversal — a dumb animal with more moral perception than the seer]
- [TC: ἰδίας παρανομίας → "his own transgression"; ἴδιος = his own, emphatic; the rebuke arose from within his own conduct; the transgression itself generated its rebuke]

---

**v.17**
These are waterless springs and mists driven by a storm, for whom the black darkness has been reserved.

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: πηγαὶ ἄνυδροι → "waterless springs"; an oxymoron — a spring is defined by its water; the false teacher promises refreshment and delivers nothing; cf. Jude 12 (νεφέλαι ἄνυδροι = "waterless clouds")]
- [TC: ὁμίχλαι ὑπὸ λαίλαπος ἐλαυνόμεναι → "mists driven by a storm"; insubstantial and directionless — driven wherever the wind forces them; no stable source, no fixed course]
- [TC: ζόφος τοῦ σκότους → "black darkness" or "gloom of utter darkness"; the double-darkness intensifier: ζόφος (Homeric, poetic darkness) + σκότους (the common NT darkness term); the doubling is deliberate and must not be collapsed to a single word; echo of ζόφου in v.4]
- [TC: τετήρηται → "has been reserved"; perfect passive — the reservation is already accomplished and stands; echo of τηρουμένους (v.4) and τηρεῖν (v.9); divine custody for punishment is the recurring motif]

---

**v.18**
For, speaking loud boasts of vanity, they entice by fleshly passions and sensuality those who are barely escaping from those who live in error.

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: ὑπέρογκα ματαιότητος → "loud boasts of vanity"; ὑπέρογκα = "over-sized/inflated/swollen words"; NASB "great swelling words of emptiness"; ματαιότητος = futility/vanity — the content behind the inflated words is hollow; cf. Jude 16]
- [TC: ἀσελγείαις → "sensuality"; third occurrence of ἀσέλγεια in ch.2 (vv.2, 7, 18); the pattern marks a defining characteristic of the false teachers and their world]
- [TC: ὀλίγως ἀποφεύγοντας → "barely escaping"; the cruelty of the targeting is acute — these are persons in the process of turning from paganism, and they are being predatorily captured at the threshold]
- [TC: ἐν πλάνῃ ἀναστρεφομένους → "those who live in error"; the pagan world from which the converts are escaping; ἀναστρέφω = to conduct one's manner of life]

---

**v.19**
promising them freedom while they themselves are slaves of corruption; for by what a man is overcome, by this he is enslaved.

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: αὐτοὶ δοῦλοι → "they themselves are slaves"; αὐτοί is emphatic — the contrast is complete: the freedom-promisers are themselves enslaved; the irony is the point]
- [TC: φθορᾶς → "corruption"; antithesis of 1:4 (escape from "the corruption in the world by lust"); those who promise escape from corruption are themselves its slaves]
- [TC: ᾧ γάρ τις ἥττηται, τούτῳ δεδούλωται → "for by what a man is overcome, by this he is enslaved"; the epigrammatic structure preserved intact — both verbs perfect passive, expressing settled, accomplished states; the instrument of defeat becomes the master; compression is the rhetorical force and must not be paraphrased]

---

**v.20**
For if, after they have escaped the defilements of the world through the knowledge of our Lord and Savior Jesus Christ, they are again entangled in them and overcome, the last state has become worse for them than the first.

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: ἐπιγνώσει → "knowledge"; ἐπίγνωσις throughout 2 Peter = full/precise knowledge (1:2, 3, 8); the same knowledge that leads to godliness marks this apostasy as informed — and therefore the condemnation as graver]
- [TC: ἐμπλακέντες → "entangled"; 2 aorist passive of ἐμπλέκω; only other NT occurrence at 2 Tim 2:4 (the soldier who must not entangle himself in civilian affairs)]
- [TC: ἡττῶνται → "overcome"; present passive — the overcoming is ongoing, contrasting with the perfect ἥττηται (v.19); the apostasy is a present, active process]
- [TC: τὰ ἔσχατα χείρονα τῶν πρώτων → "the last state…worse than the first"; cf. Matt 12:45/Luke 11:26 (the return of the unclean spirit); the principle of escalating judgment on informed apostasy]

---

**v.21**
For it would have been better for them never to have known the way of righteousness, than having known it, to turn back from the holy commandment delivered to them.

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: κρεῖττον → "better"; the counterfactual is devastating; ignorance would have been preferable to knowledge followed by deliberate apostasy — a stronger verdict than mere unbelief]
- [TC: ἐπεγνωκέναι / ἐπιγνοῦσιν → "to have known / having known"; both from ἐπιγινώσκω — the ἐπί-intensified "full knowledge" that is the goal of believers (1:2, 3, 8) becomes the ground of greater condemnation when forsaken]
- [TC: ἁγίας ἐντολῆς → "the holy commandment"; ἁγία = sacred/set apart; what they have forsaken is not incidental instruction but the entrusted sacred word]
- [TC: παραδοθείσης αὐτοῖς → "delivered to them"; tradition-transmission language (παραδίδωμι); the commandment was formally handed over and received — the apostasy is a betrayal of received trust]

---

**v.22**
It has happened to them according to the true proverb: "A dog returns to its own vomit," [OT: Prov 26:11] and, "A sow, after washing, returns to wallow in the mire."

*Translation Notes:*
- [TC: συμβέβηκεν αὐτοῖς τὸ τῆς ἀληθοῦς παροιμίας → "it has happened to them according to the true proverb"; perfect active — the proverbs are now fulfilled in their case; ἀληθοῦς = "true," not merely "common" — the proverb is genuinely, verifiably true]
- [TC: "A dog returns to its own vomit" [OT: Prov 26:11] — Peter echoes/adapts LXX Prov 26:11; ἐξέραμα here vs. ἔμετος in LXX — a synonym; the allusion is clear enough that the citation marker is warranted]
- [TC: λουσαμένη → "after washing"; the aorist participle marks the washing as prior and completed — she was washed, and returned; the baptismal echo is widely noted but left as INTERP-RISK; the text does not specify the nature of the washing]
- [TC: βορβόρου κυλισμόν → "to wallow in the mire"; βόρβορος = mud/filth/mire — the final word of the chapter; the chapter ends in the mire, answering its opening; two animals, two returns, two pollutions — the rhetoric of complete apostasy is sealed]
