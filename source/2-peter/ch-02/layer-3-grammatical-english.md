*Notes:*

**v.6**
and if he condemned the cities of Sodom and Gomorrah, having reduced them to ashes [NT-HAPAX: τεφρώσας — only NT occurrence] with overthrow, setting them as an example for those who are about to be ungodly —

*Notes:*
- τεφρώσας [NT-HAPAX]: "having reduced to ashes" — the catastrophic finality encoded in the term; not merely burned but turned to ash
- καταστροφῇ: "with overthrow" — dative of means/manner; the English "catastrophe" is a loan from this Greek word
- ὑπόδειγμα: "example" — the judgment is set forth as a standing exhibit, didactic as well as punitive; "example" preferred over "specimen/model" [INTERP-RISK: minimal]
- ἀσεβεῖν: present infinitive after μελλόντων = "those who are about to be ungodly" — the judgment is proleptic and universally applicable
- Verse ends with a dash — the conditional sentence continues into v.7

**v.7**

*Notes:*
- ἐρύσατο: aorist middle of ῥύομαι = "rescued/delivered"; the divine-rescue verb; same root recurs in v.9 (ῥύεσθαι); forms the theological spine paired with judgment (vv.4–6)
- καταπονούμενον: present participle = the wearing down was ongoing, not a single event; sustained daily suffering
- ἀθέσμων: "lawless ones" — literally "without established law/norm"; also implied in v.9
- ἀσελγείᾳ ἀναστροφῆς: "licentious conduct" — the same ἀσέλγεια linking false teachers (v.2) to Sodom (v.7)
- Verse ends with a dash — the conditional sentence continues into v.8

**v.8**
(for by sight and by hearing, the righteous one dwelling among them [NT-HAPAX: ἐγκατοικῶν — only NT occurrence] was tormenting his righteous soul day out of day [INTERP-RISK: ἡμέραν ἐξ ἡμέρας = "day from/out of day" — a Greek temporal idiom meaning "day after day"; rendered literally per instruction; Layer 4 to smooth] in their lawless works) —

*Notes:*
- βλέμματι [NT-HAPAX]: "by sight" — only NT occurrence; the noun form of βλέπω; the very act of seeing was a form of torment
- ἐγκατοικῶν [NT-HAPAX]: "dwelling among" — only NT occurrence; the compound intensifies the sense of being embedded within a hostile environment
- ἡμέραν ἐξ ἡμέρας: rendered literally as "day out of day" per instruction; the temporal idiom = unrelenting duration; note added for Layer 4
- ἐβασάνιζεν: imperfect = ongoing/repeated action; originally used of testing metals; then torture; here inner anguish
- The parenthetical structure (vv.7b–8) elaborates Lot's condition within the main conditional chain; verse ends with a dash

**v.9**
— the Lord knows how to rescue the godly ones out of trial, and to keep the unrighteous ones, being punished, for a day of judgment —

*Notes:*
- V.9 is the apodosis (conclusion) of the vv.4–9 conditional sentence — the "then" clause answering all the "if" clauses of vv.4–8
- οἶδεν: perfect with present force — the Lord's knowledge is settled and comprehensive
- ῥύεσθαι: present middle infinitive of ῥύομαι = "to rescue"; same root as ἐρύσατο (v.7); the verb of divine rescue structurally links Lot (v.7) to the general principle (v.9)
- κολαζομένους: present participle = the unrighteous are being punished while being kept, not merely waiting
- τηρεῖν: "to keep/guard/reserve" — echo of v.4 (τηρουμένους); the divine keeping applies to both angels and humans

**v.11**
Where angels, being greater in strength and power, do not bring a blasphemous judgment against them before the Lord.

*Notes:*
- ὅπου: "where" — the conjunction is adversative/contextual; "whereas" is equally valid [INTERP-RISK: minimal]
- ἰσχύϊ καὶ δυνάμει μείζονες: "greater in strength and power" — angels surpass the false teachers yet still restrain themselves; argument a fortiori
- φέρουσιν βλάσφημον κρίσιν: "bring a blasphemous judgment" — the very thing the false teachers do (v.10), the mighty angels refuse to do; the structural reversal is load-bearing
- φέρω echo: the same verb used of prophetic inspiration in 1:21 (φερόμενοι = "borne along" by the Spirit); here in the judicial sense — verbal link between ch.1 and ch.2
- παρὰ Κυρίῳ: "before the Lord" — in the Lord's presence; "before" captures the judicial setting [INTERP-RISK: minimal]

**v.12**
But these ones, as irrational animals having been born by nature for capture and destruction, blaspheming in things of which they are ignorant, will also be destroyed in their destruction —

*Notes:*
- ἄλογα ζῷα: "irrational animals" — λόγος = reason/word; these men lack reason; the irony is that they speak and teach yet are without true reason
- φυσικά: "by nature" — born for capture and destruction; they operate on pure animal instinct
- φθοράν / φθορᾷ / φθαρήσονται: triple occurrence of the φθορά root in one verse — deliberate paronomasia (wordplay); "destruction...destruction...destroyed" preserved
- βλασφημοῦντες: fourth occurrence of the blasphemy motif in ch. 2 (vv.2, 10, 11, 12)
- Verse-final dash: the description of the false teachers continues into v.13

**v.15**
Having abandoned the straight way, they went astray, having followed out the way of Balaam of Beor [VARIANT: Βεώρ = Beor, N1904 text — the Hebrew OT form (Num 22:5); Βοσόρ = Bosor, many MSS — see note], who loved the wage of unrighteousness,

*Notes:*
- VARIANT: Βεώρ (N1904) = "Beor," the Hebrew OT form; Βοσόρ = "Bosor," the Greek MSS tradition — N1904 followed per source text; the name identifies Balaam's father (Num 22:5)
- ἐξακολουθήσαντες: same verb as v.2 (future) and here aorist — "having followed out completely"; the verbal echo ties the followers (v.2) to the teachers following Balaam (v.15)
- μισθὸν ἀδικίας: "wage of unrighteousness" — exact phrase repeated from v.13; cf. Acts 1:18 (of Judas); Balaam = the archetypal mercenary prophet
- εὐθεῖαν ὁδόν: "straight way" — contrasts with "the way of Balaam"; the structural opposition of ways is load-bearing

**v.16**
but he had a rebuke for his own transgression: a voiceless beast of burden, having spoken in a human voice, restrained the madness of the prophet [NT-HAPAX: παραφρονίαν — only NT occurrence].

*Notes:*
- ἔλεγξιν ἰδίας παρανομίας: "rebuke for his own transgression" — the rebuke arose from within his own conduct; the ἰδίας (his own) is emphatic
- ὑποζύγιον: "beast of burden" — literally "under-yoke creature"; the specific term for the working donkey (Num 22)
- ἄφωνον: "voiceless" — the donkey is voiceless, yet speaks; the inversion is the rhetorical point
- παραφρονίαν [NT-HAPAX]: "madness" — only NT occurrence; the prophet's behavior = outright insanity rebuked by his own animal
- ἐκώλυσεν: "restrained" — the voiceless animal prevented/stopped the seer's madness; devastating reversal

**v.17**
These ones are waterless springs and mists being driven by a storm wind, for whom the gloom of darkness [INTERP-RISK: ζόφος τοῦ σκότους = "gloom of darkness" — a double-darkness intensifier; ζόφος is the Homeric/poetic darkness; σκότους is the common NT darkness; the doubling is deliberate and should not be collapsed to one word] has been reserved.

*Notes:*
- πηγαὶ ἄνυδροι: "waterless springs" — an oxymoron; a spring without water is the ultimate deception; promises refreshment, delivers nothing
- ὁμίχλαι: "mists" — driven by the storm wind, insubstantial and directionless; cf. Jude 12 (νεφέλαι ἄνυδροι = "waterless clouds")
- ζόφος τοῦ σκότους: "gloom of darkness" — the double-darkness intensifier; ζόφος (poetic, Homeric) + σκότους (common); cf. ζόφου in v.4
- τετήρηται: perfect passive = "has been reserved/is kept" — the reservation is accomplished and standing; echo of v.4 (τηρουμένους) and v.9 (τηρεῖν)

- ἐν πλάνῃ ἀναστρεφομένους: "those conducting themselves in error" — the pagan world from which the converts are emerging

**v.19**
promising freedom to them, themselves being slaves of corruption — for by whatever one has been overcome, by this one he has been enslaved [INTERP-RISK: ᾧ γάρ τις ἥττηται, τούτῳ δεδούλωται — the logical force of the epigrammatic construction is load-bearing: the instrument of defeat becomes the master; both verbs are perfect passives expressing settled states].

*Notes:*
- ἐλευθερίαν: "freedom" — the false promise; the irony is complete in the next clause
- αὐτοὶ δοῦλοι: "themselves slaves" — the emphatic pronoun αὐτοί placed for maximum contrast; those who promise freedom are themselves enslaved
- φθορᾶς: "corruption" — slavery to corruption; antithesis of 1:4 (ἐκφυγόντες τῆς ἐν κόσμῳ ἐν ἐπιθυμίᾳ φθορᾶς — "having escaped the corruption in the world in desire")
- ἥττηται / δεδούλωται: both perfect passives — "has been overcome / has been enslaved"; the perfects express accomplished, settled states; the epigrammatic logic compressed: overcome = enslaved

**v.20**
For if, having escaped the defilements of the world in the full knowledge of the Lord and Savior Jesus Christ, they are again overcome, having been entangled in these things, the last things have become worse for them than the first things.

*Notes:*
- μιάσματα: "defilements" — plural; many pollutions of the world; cognate with μιασμοῦ in v.10
- ἐν ἐπιγνώσει: "in the full knowledge" — ἐπίγνωσις throughout 2 Peter = full/precise knowledge (cf. 1:2, 3, 8); the same knowledge that should lead to godliness here marks the apostasy as informed and therefore graver
- ἐμπλακέντες: "having been entangled" — 2 aor pass participle of ἐμπλέκω; only other NT occurrence: 2 Tim 2:4 (the soldier must not become entangled)
- ἡττῶνται: present passive (contrast with perfect ἥττηται in v.19) = the overcoming is ongoing
- τὰ ἔσχατα χείρονα τῶν πρώτων: "the last things worse than the first things" — cf. Matt 12:45/Luke 11:26 (same formula after the return of the unclean spirit); the principle of escalating judgment on apostasy

**v.21**
For it was better for them not to have fully known the way of righteousness than, having fully known it, to turn back from the holy commandment delivered to them.

*Notes:*
- κρεῖττον: "better" — the counterfactual is devastating; ignorance would have been preferable to knowledge followed by apostasy
- ἐπεγνωκέναι / ἐπιγνοῦσιν: both from ἐπιγινώσκω — "to have fully known / having fully known"; the ἐπί- intensifier preserved throughout; the full knowledge (ἐπίγνωσις) that is the goal of believers (1:2, 3, 8) becomes the basis for greater condemnation when forsaken
- ὑποστρέψαι: "to turn back" — the verbal image of reversing direction; apostasy as a turning back from
- ἁγίας ἐντολῆς: "holy commandment" — what they have forsaken is sacred entrusted instruction; παραδοθείσης αὐτοῖς ("delivered/handed over to them") carries the tradition-transmission nuance

**v.22**
The word of the true proverb has happened to them: [OT/PROVERBIAL ALLUSION: Prov 26:11 LXX] "A dog having returned to its own vomit" — and, "A washed sow to wallowing in mud."

*Notes:*
- συμβέβηκεν αὐτοῖς: "has happened to them" — perfect active; the proverbs are now fulfilled in their case
- ἀληθοῦς παροιμίας: "true proverb" — the proverb is introduced as genuinely true (ἀληθοῦς); two proverbs are cited
- [OT/PROVERBIAL ALLUSION: Prov 26:11]: "A dog having returned to its own vomit" — Peter echoes/adapts LXX Prov 26:11 (κύων ἐπιστρέψας ἐπὶ τὸν ἑαυτοῦ ἔμετον); ἐξέραμα (vomit) here vs. ἔμετος in LXX
- Second proverb (the washed sow) has no direct OT source; may reflect broader proverbial tradition; the baptismal echo (washing → return to mire) is widely noted but left for Layer 4 interpretation [INTERP-RISK: to draw the baptismal connection is interpretive]
- βορβόρου: "mud/mire/filth" — the final word of the chapter; the chapter ends in the mire, answering its opening — those who seemed to escape return to defilement
- The double proverb (dog + pig) forms the rhetorical climax: two animals, two returns to their respective pollution — complete apostasy
