Editorial Conventions (Non-Variant Differences)

The following inter-tradition differences are editorial and are not recorded as variants below: - Capitalization of divine titles (Θεοῦ/θεοῦ, Κυρίου/κυρίου, Πνεύματος/πνεύματος): N1904 capitalizes; SBLGNT/NA28/WH/RP print lower-case. Noted here once. - Punctuation (comma / semicolon / em-dash placement): editors' reading aids; not textual. - Elision apostrophes (δι᾽ vs διʼ vs δι'): typographic. - Iota subscript presence/absence: orthographic.

All other differences — word presence/absence, word choice, word order, spelling, and case changes — are tracked as variants.

  3. verb number (singular → plural). grammatical. Plural στοιχεῖα naturally expects plural verb; the singular in the eclectic text is the harder reading (possibly constructed ad sensum with an implied collective or with κόσμος)
  4. lexical/textual — three entirely different readings for the verse-ending verb. highly substantive — this is one of the hardest textual problems in the NT.
