2 Peter 1 — L4 Phrase-for-Phrase

2 Peter, Chapter 1

Role: Phrase-level equivalence — bilingual biblical linguist Tradition: NASB 1995 / ESV comparable Source: Layer 3 (grammatical English) + Layer 1 (Greek) Generated by: Claude (Executive Director) Date: 2026-03-19

Layer 4 Integrity Rule: Translate complete phrases and idioms as units. Handle Greek idioms with their English functional equivalent. Minimal interpretive expansion — meaning transfer, not paraphrase. Every translation choice traceable to a lexical or grammatical decision. Flag significant choices with [TC: reason].

v.1 Simeon Peter, a servant and apostle of Jesus Christ, to those who have received a faith of equal standing with ours in the righteousness of our God and Savior Jesus Christ:

[TC: δοῦλος = "servant" in epistolary address per NASB/ESV convention; lexically "slave" is more precise but "servant" is standard in formal letter greetings] [TC: λαχοῦσιν = "have received" — divine-allotment force of λαγχάνω absorbed into "received"; "have been allotted" would be more literal but awkward] [TC: ἰσότιμον = "of equal standing" — NT hapax; captures honor-equivalence between recipients and apostolic circle; NASB uses "of the same kind," ESV "of equal standing"] [TC: ἐν δικαιοσύνῃ = "in the righteousness" — locative en; ESV uses "by the righteousness" (instrumental); both defensible; "in" preferred as the faith is grounded within God's righteousness] [TC: Granville Sharp Rule applied — "our God and Savior Jesus Christ" = one person; theologically significant; grammatically unambiguous]

v.2 Grace and peace be multiplied to you in the knowledge of God and of Jesus our Lord.

[TC: ἐπιγνώσει = "knowledge" — standard English rendering; the ἐπί-prefix distinction from γνῶσις is preserved in Layer 7A; NASB uses "true knowledge"] [TC: πληθυνθείη = optative wish; "be multiplied" is natural English for this prayer form]

v.3 seeing that His divine power has granted to us everything pertaining to life and godliness, through the true knowledge of Him who called us by His own glory and excellence,

[TC: Ὡς causal = "seeing that" per NASB; "since" (ESV) equally valid; "seeing that" slightly more formal] [TC: subject "His" must be supplied; referent is Christ (from vv.1–2); verse is a subordinate clause completing in v.4] [TC: πάντα = "everything" (SBLGNT anarthrous); N1904 τὰ πάντα = "all things" — minimal meaning difference; "everything" follows SBLGNT] [TC: ἐπίγνωσις = "true knowledge" — NASB convention to distinguish from γνῶσις] [TC: ἀρετή = "excellence" — NASB uses "excellence" to distinguish from δόξα (glory); ESV uses "glory" for both, which obscures the two distinct terms in the Greek]

v.4 through which He has granted to us His precious and magnificent promises, so that through them you may become partakers of the divine nature, having escaped the corruption that is in the world by lust.

[TC: τίμια καὶ μέγιστα = "precious and magnificent" — NASB rendering; "very great and precious" (ESV reversal) also valid] [TC: θείας κοινωνοὶ φύσεως = "partakers of the divine nature" — this is the standard and only reasonable rendering; unique theological phrase in the NT] [TC: φθορᾶς = "corruption" rather than "decay" — moral corruption in context] [TC: ἐν ἐπιθυμίᾳ = "by lust" — instrumental; the corruption is characterized/driven by desire; "through lust" equally valid]

v.5 Now for this very reason also, applying all diligence, in your faith supply moral excellence, and in your moral excellence, knowledge;

[TC: καὶ αὐτὸ τοῦτο δὲ = "now for this very reason also" — NASB rendering of the intensive demonstrative; captures the inferential force] [TC: παρεισενέγκαντες πᾶσαν σπουδήν = "applying all diligence" — NASB rendering; the compound verb's force (bringing in alongside) absorbed] [TC: ἐπιχορηγήσατε = "supply" — the lavish-provision connotation of the root (χορός) noted; "provide" is flatter] [TC: ἀρετήν = "moral excellence" — consistent with v.3 choice; NASB]

v.6 and in your knowledge, self-control, and in your self-control, perseverance, and in your perseverance, godliness;

[TC: ἐγκράτεια = "self-control" — standard; Greek Stoic virtue term adopted into Christian vocabulary] [TC: ὑπομονή = "perseverance" — endurance under circumstances; distinct from μακροθυμία (patience with persons); NASB/ESV both use "perseverance"] [TC: εὐσέβεια = "godliness" — standard; the term is central to the Pastoral Epistles and 2 Peter]

v.7 and in your godliness, brotherly kindness, and in your brotherly kindness, love.

[TC: φιλαδελφία = "brotherly kindness" — NASB; ESV uses "brotherly affection"; both capture the sibling-love nuance] [TC: ἀγάπη = "love" — the crown virtue; no further qualification needed; the sorites chain ends here]

v.8 For if these qualities are yours and are increasing, they render you neither useless nor unfruitful in the true knowledge of our Lord Jesus Christ.

[TC: ὑπάρχοντα καὶ πλεονάζοντα = "are yours and are increasing" — English rendering of two present active participles; both conditions required] [TC: ἀργούς = "useless" — NASB; "ineffective" (ESV) also valid; ἀ + ἔργον = without-work] [TC: ἀκάρπους = "unfruitful" — standard rendering] [TC: εἰς τὴν ἐπίγνωσιν = "in the true knowledge" — eis expressing sphere/reference]

v.9 For whoever lacks these qualities is blind and short-sighted, having forgotten his purification from his former sins.

[TC: τυφλός ἐστιν μυωπάζων = "is blind and short-sighted" — the paradox preserved; "short-sighted" captures NT hapax μυωπάζω better than "squinting"; "blind" may describe total spiritual blindness, "short-sighted" its particular character] [TC: λήθην λαβών = "having forgotten" — Greek idiom resolved to English; the literal "having taken forgetfulness" would be unnatural] [TC: καθαρισμοῦ = "purification" — the cleansing of baptism/conversion implied] [TC: LEXICAL VARIANT — "sins" (ἁμαρτία, N1904/NA28, Str. 266) followed here; SBLGNT reads ἁμαρτημάτων (ἁμάρτημα, Str. 265 = "sinful acts/deeds"). If SBLGNT followed: "...from his former sinful acts." Both are defensible; N1904/NA28 reading used per critical consensus.]

v.10 Therefore, brothers, be all the more diligent to make your calling and election certain; for as long as you practice these things, you will never stumble;

[TC: ἀδελφοί = "brothers" — standard epistolary address; vocative] [TC: βεβαίαν...ποιεῖσθαι = "make...certain" — NASB rendering; "confirm" (ESV) also valid; middle-voice reflexive force retained in "make [for yourselves]"] [TC: κλῆσιν καὶ ἐκλογήν = "calling and election" — standard theological terms; divine sovereignty assumed alongside human diligence] [TC: οὐ μή + subjunctive = "will never" — strongest Greek negation rendered as emphatic English future negative]

v.11 for in this way the entrance into the eternal kingdom of our Lord and Savior Jesus Christ will be abundantly provided to you.

[TC: εἴσοδος = "entrance" — NASB; the triumphal/royal entry connotation of the word is preserved without over-interpretation] [TC: ἐπιχορηγηθήσεται = "will be abundantly provided" — future passive; deliberate echo of v.5 ἐπιχορηγήσατε (you supply → it will be supplied to you)] [TC: Granville Sharp Rule again: τοῦ κυρίου...καὶ σωτῆρος = one person, as in v.1]

v.12 Therefore, I will always be ready to remind you of these things, even though you know them and have been established in the truth which is present with you.

[TC: μελλήσω = "will be ready" — future of μέλλω; NASB rendering; "I intend" (ESV) is also valid] [TC: VARIANT: some MSS read οὐκ ἀμελήσω = "I will not neglect" — entirely different word; SBLGNT/NA28 reading followed] [TC: ἐστηριγμένους = "have been established" — perfect passive participle; completed action with ongoing state] [TC: τῇ παρούσῃ ἀληθείᾳ = "the truth which is present with you" — participial adjective resolved]

v.13 I consider it right, as long as I am in this earthly dwelling, to stir you up by reminder,

[TC: σκήνωμα = "earthly dwelling" — the tent/body metaphor; "this body" (NLT) would be too interpretive; "earthly dwelling" preserves the image] [TC: διεγείρειν = "stir up" — intensive compound; more than simply "remind"] [TC: ἐν ὑπομνήσει = "by reminder" — instrumental; the letter itself is the means]

v.14 knowing that the laying aside of this earthly dwelling is imminent, as also our Lord Jesus Christ has made clear to me.

[TC: ἀπόθεσις = "laying aside" — NASB rendering; the clothing/tent metaphor preserved] [TC: ταχινή = "imminent" — NASB; captures the urgency; "soon" is also valid] [TC: ἐδήλωσέν μοι = "has made clear to me" — reference to John 21:18–19 implicit; Layer 4 does not interpret the referent]

v.15 And I will also be diligent that at any time after my departure you will be able to call these things to mind.

[TC: σπουδάσω = "will be diligent" — future active; Peter's personal resolve] [TC: ἔξοδον = "departure" — NASB; preserves the Exodus resonance without over-commentary; "decease" (KJV) is archaic] [TC: τὴν τούτων μνήμην ποιεῖσθαι = "call these things to mind" — the letter itself as the memorial medium]

v.16 For we did not follow cleverly devised myths when we made known to you the power and coming of our Lord Jesus Christ, but we were eyewitnesses of His majesty.

[TC: σεσοφισμένοις μύθοις = "cleverly devised myths" — NASB; the perfect passive participle "devised/crafted" is important] [TC: παρουσίαν = "coming" — the technical early Christian term for Christ's return; NASB/ESV use "coming"; "presence" would be too broad here] [TC: ἐπόπται = "eyewitnesses" — highest-grade mystery-religion initiates; rendered as "eyewitnesses" per standard; the polemic subversion of the term is a Layer 7D matter] [TC: μεγαλειότητος = "majesty" — NASB; the Transfiguration context makes this the referent]

v.17 For when He received honor and glory from God the Father, such a declaration as this was made to Him by the Majestic Glory: "This is My beloved Son with whom I am well-pleased"—

[TC: φωνῆς ἐνεχθείσης = "declaration...was made" — NASB rendering; the phero participle's "borne/carried" sense absorbed into "made"; preserving "borne" would sound odd in English here but the verbal echo is documented] [TC: μεγαλοπρεποῦς δόξης = "Majestic Glory" — NT hapax; circumlocution for God preserved as title-phrase per NASB convention] [TC: word order of the Transfiguration quotation follows SBLGNT; Synoptic parallels have slightly different forms] [TC: em-dash preserves the mid-sentence structure of the Greek original]

v.18 and we ourselves heard this declaration made from heaven when we were with Him on the holy mountain.

[TC: ἡμεῖς = "we ourselves" — emphatic pronoun; eyewitness stress preserved] [TC: ἐνεχθεῖσαν = "made from heaven" — phero echo #2; "borne from heaven" is more literal; "made" chosen for natural English] [TC: ἁγίῳ ὄρει = "the holy mountain" — Transfiguration location; Mount Hermon or Tabor per tradition; Layer 4 does not specify]

v.19 We have also the prophetic word made more sure, to which you do well to pay attention as to a lamp shining in a dark place, until the day dawns and the morning star arises in your hearts.

[TC: βεβαιότερον = "more sure" — comparative; NASB rendering; the "more sure than what?" question is a legitimate exegetical crux, noted but not resolved here] [TC: αὐχμηρῷ = "dark" — NT hapax; "squalid" or "murky" also possible; "dark" is the most natural English] [TC: φωσφόρος = "morning star" — NT hapax; NASB/ESV both use "morning star"; the Venus/dawn imagery is eschatological] [TC: ἐν ταῖς καρδίαις ὑμῶν = "in your hearts" — the interior/spiritual nature of the eschatological dawn is textual, not commentary]

v.20 But know this first of all, that no prophecy of Scripture is a matter of one's own interpretation,

[TC: τοῦτο πρῶτον γινώσκοντες = "know this first of all" — NASB rendering; the participial phrase made imperative in English context] [TC: ἰδίας ἐπιλύσεως οὐ γίνεται = "is a matter of one's own interpretation" — NASB rendering of this crux] [TC: INTERP-RISK — CRUX UNRESOLVED: (a) authorial sense: prophets did not privately interpret/originate their own prophecies (supported by v.21 context); (b) readerly sense: no reader should privately interpret Scripture. Context of v.21 ("not by human will...moved by the Holy Spirit") strongly supports (a). Layer 4 follows NASB in leaving the phrasing open; Layer 7B/7F will resolve.]

v.21 for no prophecy was ever made by an act of human will, but men moved by the Holy Spirit spoke from God.

[TC: ἠνέχθη = "was made" — NASB rendering; phero echo #3a; "was borne" is more literal but "was made" is natural English] [TC: φερόμενοι = "moved by the Holy Spirit" — NASB; present passive participle = ongoing continuous action; "carried along" (ESV) is more vivid and lexically closer to phero] [TC: ἀπὸ θεοῦ = "from God" — SBLGNT/NA28 reading; p72 and B read ἅγιοι θεοῦ = "holy men of God"; SBLGNT reading followed]
