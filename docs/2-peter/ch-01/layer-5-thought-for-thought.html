2 Peter 1 — L5 Thought-for-Thought

2 Peter, Chapter 1

Role: Functional equivalence — meaning transfer in natural English Tradition: NIV / CSB comparable Source: Layer 4 (phrase-for-phrase) + Layer 1 (Greek) Generated by: Claude (Executive Director) Date: 2026-03-19

Layer 5 Integrity Rule: Prioritize natural English expression of the complete thought. Restructure sentences as needed for clarity. Replace cultural idioms with contemporary equivalents where meaning would be lost. Preserve theological precision — do not soften or intensify the text's own claims. No doctrinal insertions or commentary. Audience: educated adult English speaker with no biblical background.

v.1 From Simeon Peter, a servant and apostle of Jesus Christ. To those who have received a faith as precious as ours through the righteousness of our God and Savior Jesus Christ:

v.2 May grace and peace be yours in abundance as you grow in the knowledge of God and of Jesus our Lord.

v.3 By his divine power, God has given us everything we need for life and godliness through our knowledge of him who called us to share in his own glory and goodness.

v.4 Through that glory and goodness he has given us his very great and precious promises, so that through them you may participate in the divine nature, having escaped the corruption in the world caused by evil desires.

v.5 For this very reason, make every effort to add to your faith goodness; and to goodness, knowledge;

v.6 and to knowledge, self-control; and to self-control, perseverance; and to perseverance, godliness;

v.7 and to godliness, mutual affection; and to mutual affection, love.

v.8 For if you possess these qualities in increasing measure, they will keep you from being ineffective and unproductive in your knowledge of our Lord Jesus Christ.

v.9 But whoever does not have them is nearsighted and blind, having forgotten that they have been cleansed from their past sins.

v.10 Therefore, brothers and sisters, make every effort to confirm your calling and election. For if you do these things, you will never stumble,

v.11 and you will receive a rich welcome into the eternal kingdom of our Lord and Savior Jesus Christ.

v.12 So I will always remind you of these things, even though you know them and are firmly established in the truth you now have.

v.13 I think it is right to refresh your memory as long as I live in the tent of this body,

v.14 because I know that I will soon put it aside, as our Lord Jesus Christ has made clear to me.

v.15 And I will make every effort to see that after my departure you will always be able to remember these things.

v.16 For we did not follow cleverly invented stories when we told you about the coming of our Lord Jesus Christ in power, but we were eyewitnesses of his majesty.

v.17 He received honor and glory from God the Father when the voice came to him from the Majestic Glory, saying, "This is my Son, whom I love; with him I am well pleased."

v.18 We ourselves heard this voice that came from heaven when we were with him on the sacred mountain.

v.19 We also have the prophetic message as something completely reliable, and you will do well to pay attention to it, as to a light shining in a dark place, until the day dawns and the morning star rises in your hearts.

v.20 Above all, you must understand that no prophecy of Scripture came about by the prophet's own interpretation of things.

v.21 For prophecy never had its origin in the human will, but prophets, though human, spoke from God as they were carried along by the Holy Spirit.

4 "having escaped the corruption...by lust" "having escaped the corruption...caused by evil desires" "evil desires" more accessible than "lust" for modern audience; meaning preserved
5 "supply moral excellence" "add to your faith goodness" NIV convention; "goodness" for ἀρετή at L5; less technical, more accessible
7 "brotherly kindness" "mutual affection" More accessible to non-church audience; φιλαδελφία = affection for fellow believers
8 "neither useless nor unfruitful" "ineffective and unproductive" NIV rendering; same meaning, natural English
9 "having forgotten his purification from his former sins" "having forgotten that they have been cleansed from their past sins" Clarifies the referent of the forgetting; "cleansed" for καθαρισμός
10 "brothers" "brothers and sisters" Modern inclusive convention; ἀδελφοί as generic address includes all believers
11 "entrance...will be abundantly provided" "will receive a rich welcome" NIV rendering; "rich welcome" captures πλουσίως ἐπιχορηγηθήσεται naturally

20 "is a matter of one's own interpretation" "came about by the prophet's own interpretation" Follows authorial-sense reading of the crux (supported by v.21 context)
