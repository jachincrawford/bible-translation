This letter comes from Simon Peter — a servant of Jesus Christ and one of his official representatives. [v.1] I'm writing to you who share the same precious, God-given faith that we do. The way we got it and the way you got it are no different — it came to us all purely because of what our God and Savior Jesus Christ has done. [v.2] My prayer for you is that God's grace and peace would keep multiplying in your life as you grow in your understanding of him and of Jesus our Lord.

Here's the foundation everything else rests on: [v.3] God's own power has already supplied you with absolutely everything you need to live a full, deeply godly life. He did this through knowing him — through the real, personal knowledge of the one who called you to himself. And the way he called you says everything about who he is: magnificent in his greatness and beautiful in his moral character. [v.4] Out of that same greatness and beauty, he has made extraordinary promises to us — promises so significant that through them you can actually begin to share in God's own nature, his own life and character. And as you step into that, you leave behind the moral decay that comes from living only for what your own desires want.

So here's the response all of that calls for — give it everything you've got. [v.5] Start with the faith you've been given, and actively build on it: add goodness to your faith. Then add understanding to your goodness. [v.6] Add self-discipline to your understanding. Add the ability to keep going when things get hard to your self-discipline. Add deep reverence for God to that perseverance. [v.7] Then add warm love for your fellow believers to your reverence for God. And finally, let all of it be crowned by love — genuine, outward-reaching love for everyone around you.

The payoff of all this is real and measurable: [v.8] when these qualities are actually growing in you, your relationship with Jesus Christ won't go stale. You'll be genuinely productive and fruitful, not just spinning your wheels. [v.9] But if someone isn't developing these qualities, it's a sign they've gone spiritually nearsighted — they can only see what's right in front of them, the immediate and the visible. Even worse, they've forgotten something that should be impossible to forget: that God already wiped the slate clean of everything they did wrong before they knew him.

This is why you need to keep pressing forward. [v.10] Friends, put everything into making sure that God's call on your life and his choice of you are things you can feel certain about — not through doubt, but through the evidence of how you're actually living. Do these things consistently and you simply will not fall. [v.11] Instead, what awaits you is a glorious, full welcome into the everlasting kingdom of our Lord and Savior Jesus Christ. Nothing stingy about it — it will be rich and abundant.

Now, you might think you've heard all this before, and you have — [v.12] but I'm going to keep bringing it up anyway. You already know this truth and you're standing firm in it. That's actually exactly why I keep reminding you — not because you're ignorant, but because these truths are too important to let drift. [v.13] I think stirring you up, keeping your memory sharp, is my responsibility for as long as I'm alive. [v.14] And I know I won't be around much longer. Jesus himself made it clear to me that my time is coming soon. [v.15] So I'm working hard to make sure that even after I'm gone, you'll have a reliable way to keep calling these things to mind.

When we told you about Jesus Christ coming back in power, we weren't making things up. [v.16] We didn't pass along creative religious stories or carefully crafted fictions. We saw it with our own eyes. We were actual witnesses of his overwhelming greatness — not spectators of a performance, but people who were there. [v.17] Here's what I mean: we were on the mountain with him when God the Father honored him with glory. We heard the voice come — that magnificent, awe-filling divine presence — and it said these words: "This is my Son, my beloved, and I couldn't be more pleased with him." [v.18] We heard that voice ourselves. It came straight from heaven. We were standing right there with him when it happened, on that holy mountain. This is not a story we inherited — it is our testimony.

And here's what that experience did: [v.19] it made us grip the ancient prophetic writings even more firmly. Those writings have always been the reliable lamp in a dark room — the only light you have until the full daylight breaks through and the morning star rises inside you. Pay close attention to them. [v.20] But first, understand this about how Scripture works: the prophets weren't just giving their own opinions or personal interpretations of things. [v.21] No prophet ever sat down and decided on his own to write what he wrote. These were real human beings, yes — but they were carried along, borne forward, by God's own Holy Spirit. God was the source of what they spoke. That is what makes it Scripture, and that is why you can trust it with your life.
