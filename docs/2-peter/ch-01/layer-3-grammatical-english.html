v.1 Simeon Peter, a slave and apostle of Jesus Christ, to those who have obtained by lot a faith equal in honor to ours in the righteousness of our God and Savior Jesus Christ:

Notes: - "slave" preserved over "servant" — doulos is the standard gloss; Layer 4 may choose to soften - Granville Sharp Rule: single article tou governs both Theou and Soteros through kai — "our God and Savior" = one referent (Jesus Christ); [INTERP-RISK: theological conclusion, though grammatically clear] - "equal in honor to ours" — isotimos is hapax; gloss follows lexical range (equal in value/honor); "ours" required by Greek hemin (dative of reference) - "have obtained by lot" — 2nd aorist active participle of lagchano; "by lot" preserves the divine-allotment connotation of the verb

v.2 Grace to you and peace be multiplied in the full knowledge of God and of Jesus our Lord.

Notes: - "may be multiplied" → "be multiplied" — Greek optative (wish/prayer) rendered as English third-person imperative-like wish; "may be multiplied" is equally valid; no INTERP-RISK - epignosis = "full knowledge" (distinguished from gnosis; preserved throughout per Layer 1 note)

v.3 Since his divine power has granted to us all things [INTERP-RISK: N1904 reads "all the things"] pertaining to life and godliness through the full knowledge of the one who called us by his own glory and virtue,

Notes: - "Since" — hos here is causal, not comparative; chosen over "as" to preserve causal force [INTERP-RISK: could be "inasmuch as"] - "has granted" — perfect passive participle dedoremenes rendered as English perfect active for readability; passive-to-active conversion required in English - "all things" (anarthrous, SBLGNT/NA28) vs. "all the things" (articular, N1904) — SBLGNT reading followed; N1904 variant noted - "by his own glory and virtue" — idia doxe kai arete (dative of means/manner); dative reading followed per SBLGNT/N1904/NA28; variant (genitive "through glory") noted - Verse ends without main verb — Greek sentence continues into v.4; English requires continuation; [INTERP-RISK: sentence structure preserved as subordinate clause leading to v.4]

v.4 through which he has granted to us the precious and very great promises, so that through these you might become partakers of the divine nature, having escaped the corruption that is in the world in desire.

Notes: - "through which" — di' hon, relative pronoun referring back to glory and virtue (v.3) - "precious and very great" — timia kai megista; superlative megista = "greatest/very great" - "has granted" — perfect middle deponent dedoretai - "partakers of the divine nature" — theias koinonoi phuseos; Greek word order preserved (divine / partakers / of-nature); English requires slight reorder for readability [INTERP-RISK: minimal] - "the corruption that is in the world in desire" — literal rendering of tes en to kosmo en epithumia phthoras; the double-en (in the world, in desire) is intentionally preserved; Layer 4 will smooth - "having escaped" — 2nd aorist active participle; antecedent action to becoming partakers

v.5 And for this very reason, having brought in alongside all diligence, supply abundantly in your faith virtue, and in virtue knowledge,

Notes: - "for this very reason" — kai auto touto de; the intensive pronoun auto touto de is idiomatic; literal gloss ("and this-very this but") rendered as English idiom [INTERP-RISK: idiomatic resolution] - "having brought in alongside all diligence" — pareisenenkantes pasan spouden; the compound verb pareis-phero = "bring in alongside/additionally"; awkward in English but preserved at L3; "all diligence" = pasan spouden - "supply abundantly" — epichoregisate (aorist active imperative); the verb implies lavish, generous provision; "abundantly" added to capture the cho- root [INTERP-RISK: slight expansion, but lexically warranted] - Sorites chain begins: faith supplies → virtue → knowledge (continues vv.6-7)

v.6 and in knowledge self-control, and in self-control endurance, and in endurance godliness,

Notes: - Identical syntactic pattern to v.5 repeated three times; preserved exactly - No textual variants; no INTERP-RISK flags needed

v.7 and in godliness brotherly love, and in brotherly love love.

Notes: - agapen as final term; no further chain link — agape is the telos, not a rung - "brotherly love" for philadelphia; "love" for agape; distinction preserved

v.8 For these things existing in you and abounding do not render you idle nor unfruitful with respect to the full knowledge of our Lord Jesus Christ.

Notes: - "existing...and abounding" — two present active participles (huparchonta, pleonazonta) both nominative plural neuter, agreeing with tauta (these things); English requires finite verb structure - "do not render you idle nor unfruitful" — ouk argous oude akarpous kathistesin; double negative preserved - "with respect to the full knowledge" — eis ten epignosin; eis used in pregnant sense; "with respect to" more literal than "toward" here [INTERP-RISK: minor]

v.9 For the one in whom these things are not present is blind, being nearsighted, having taken forgetfulness of the cleansing of his formerly [committed] sins.

Notes: - "the one in whom" — ho relative pronoun resolved to English phrase; Greek relative clause structure preserved [INTERP-RISK: minimal restructuring] - "being nearsighted" — muopazon (NT hapax); preserved as literal rendering; Layer 4 may refine - "having taken forgetfulness" — lethen labon = Greek idiom for "having forgotten"; kept literal at L3 to preserve the idiom; [INTERP-RISK: Layer 4 must decide whether to render as idiom or literal] - "formerly committed" — [INTERP-RISK: "committed" is supplied to complete "sins" as an English noun phrase; Greek has palai hamartion = "formerly of-sins"] - LEXICAL VARIANT: "sins" follows N1904/NA28 (hamartia, Str. 266); SBLGNT reads hamartematon (hamartema, Str. 265 = sinful acts/deeds). Layer 4 must adjudicate. If SBLGNT followed: "...cleansing of his formerly committed sinful acts."

v.10 Therefore, brothers, be all the more diligent to make your calling and election firm; for doing these things you shall by no means ever stumble.

Notes: - "all the more" — mallon rendered as "all the more" given inferential context (dio mallon) - "make...firm" — bebaian...poieisthai (present middle infinitive with adjective); reflexive force of middle preserved in English "make [for yourselves]" though English doesn't require pronoun - ou me + subjunctive = strongest Greek negation; "shall by no means ever" preserves the double negative force

v.11 For in this way the entrance into the eternal kingdom of our Lord and Savior Jesus Christ will be richly supplied to you.

Notes: - "in this way" — houtos - "will be richly supplied" — epichoregethestai (future passive); deliberate echo of epichoregisate (v.5 aorist active imperative); you supply → it will be supplied to you - "entrance" — eisodos; royal/triumphal entry connotation preserved in word choice - Granville Sharp Rule applies again: tou Kuriou...kai Soteros = same referent as v.1

v.12 Therefore I intend always to remind you concerning these things, although you know them and are established in the present truth.

Notes: - "I intend" — melleso (future active indicative); chosen over "I am about to" for English naturalness [INTERP-RISK: "I am about to always remind" is awkward; "I intend" captures the sense] - VARIANT: some MSS read ouk ameleso = "I will not neglect to remind you" — entirely different construction - "the present truth" — te parous aletheia; "present" is a participial adjective (parousa from pareimi)

v.13 And I consider it right, as long as I am in this tent, to stir you up by way of reminder,

Notes: - "as long as" — eph' hoson; rendered as English temporal clause - "tent" — skenoma; body as temporary tent/dwelling; "tabernacle" is also valid but more ecclesial; [INTERP-RISK: Layer 4 decision] - "by way of reminder" — en hupomnései; instrumental en

v.14 knowing that the laying aside of my tent is swift, just as also our Lord Jesus Christ made clear to me.

Notes: - "laying aside" — apothesis; "putting off" also valid; "laying aside" chosen as slightly more natural English while preserving the clothing/garment metaphor - "swift" — tachine; could also be "soon/imminent"; "swift" preserves the adjective force - "made clear to me" — edelosen moi; reference to John 21:18-19 implicit but not stated (Layer 3 does not interpret)

v.15 And I will also be diligent at all times to have you make remembrance of these things after my departure.

Notes: - "at all times" — hekastote - "to have you make remembrance" — echein humas...mnemen poieisthai; the double infinitive construction is awkward but preserved; the letter itself (2 Peter) is likely the vehicle of this ongoing remembrance [Layer 3 does not interpret this] - "departure" — exodos; same word as Moses's "exodus"; the word used of Jesus's death at the Transfiguration (Luke 9:31); resonance noted but not interpreted at L3

v.16 For not having followed cleverly devised myths did we make known to you the power and coming of our Lord Jesus Christ, but having become eyewitnesses of that one's majesty.

Notes: - Greek word order preserved as much as possible (negative clause first, then positive) - "cleverly devised myths" — sesophismenois muthois; perfect passive participle attributive - "eyewitnesses" — epoptai; highest-grade mystery-religion initiates term repurposed by Peter; "eyewitnesses" is the semantic equivalent; the mystery-religion connotation is a Layer 7D matter - "that one's majesty" — tes ekeinou megaleiohetos; ekeinou is demonstrative used as emphatic pronoun; "that one's" is deliberately distant/solemn

v.17 For having received from God the Father honor and glory — a voice having been borne to him of such a kind from the Majestic Glory: "This is my Son, my Beloved, in whom I was well-pleased" —

Notes: - "having been borne" — enegktheises (aorist passive participle of phero); phero echo #1; literal rendering intentional - "Majestic Glory" — tes megaloprepous doxes (NT hapax); rendered as title/circumlocution for God; "Majestic" for megaloprepous - Transfiguration quotation: word order follows SBLGNT (houtos estin ho huios mou ho agapetos = "This is my Son, my Beloved"); some MSS and Synoptic parallels have different order [noted, not adjudicated] - Verse ends with dash — the main verb (enegktheises) governs this genitive absolute clause; sentence continues structurally into v.18

v.18 and this voice we ourselves heard borne from heaven, being with him on the holy mountain.

Notes: - "we ourselves" — hemeis (emphatic pronoun); eyewitness emphasis - "borne from heaven" — enegktheisan (aorist passive participle); phero echo #2; preserves the verbal link to v.17 - "being with him" — sun auto ontes; present participle of eimi

v.19 And we have the prophetic word as more sure, to which you do well to pay attention as to a lamp shining in a dark place, until the day dawns and the morning star rises in your hearts,

Notes: - "more sure" — bebaioheron (comparative adjective); [INTERP-RISK: "more sure than what?" is a legitimate exegetical question (than the Transfiguration experience? or: the prophecies are confirmed by the Transfiguration?); Layer 3 does not resolve this] - "dark place" — auchmero topo (NT hapax auchmeros); "dark" chosen over "gloomy/murky" - "morning star" — phosphoros (NT hapax); "morning star" chosen over "light-bearer"; eschatological connotation preserved without interpretation - "in your hearts" — en tais kardiais humon; the inner/spiritual dawn is textual; not commentary

v.20 knowing this first, that every prophecy of Scripture does not come from one's own interpretation,

Notes: - "knowing this first" — touto proton ginoskontes; participial phrase introducing doctrinal ground for v.19 exhortation - "does not come from one's own interpretation" — idias epiluseos ou ginetai; epilusis is NT hapax - [INTERP-RISK: CRUX INTERPRETUM — "one's own" (idias) could refer to: (a) the prophet's own interpretation (the prophet did not originate/interpret his own prophecy — authorial sense); or (b) the reader's own private interpretation (no private interpretation — readerly sense). Context of v.21 strongly favors (a): the prophet was passive, borne by the Spirit. Layer 3 preserves the ambiguity with "one's own" without resolution. Layer 4 must decide.]

v.21 for prophecy was never borne by the will of man, but men from God spoke being borne along by the Holy Spirit.

Notes: - "was never borne" — enechthe...pote; aorist passive indicative of phero; phero echo #3a; "borne" preserved deliberately to echo vv.17-18 - "being borne along" — pheromenoi; present passive participle; phero echo #3b; present tense = ongoing/continuous action during prophetic speaking - "men from God" — apo Theou anthropoi; SBLGNT/NA28 reading followed - VARIANT: p72 and B read hagioi Theou = "holy men of God" (without apo); would read "...holy men of God spoke being borne along by the Holy Spirit." Layer 4 must adjudicate. - The three phero echoes (vv.17, 18, 21) are now complete: the voice at the Transfiguration was borne to Jesus → we heard it borne from heaven → prophecy was borne by the Spirit.
